# Teen girl struck by vehicle near Chesterfield elementary school

**TL;DR:** A 14-year-old girl was struck by a vehicle near Crenshaw Elementary School in Midlothian after stepping into traffic on Bailey Bridge Road on Tuesday evening.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** 14-year-old girl
- **What:** Struck by vehicle after stepping into traffic
- **When:** Tuesday, June 16, 2026, around 7:30 p.m.
- **Where:** 11901 Bailey Bridge Road near Crenshaw Elementary School, Midlothian

## The story

On the evening of Tuesday, June 16, 2026, Chesterfield County police responded to a crash involving a pedestrian near Crenshaw Elementary School, located at 11901 Bailey Bridge Road in Midlothian. A 14-year-old girl was struck by a vehicle in the 11900 block of Bailey Bridge Road after intentionally stepping out onto the roadway, according to police. The driver remained at the scene and is cooperating with the investigation.

The teenage girl was transported from the scene by MedFlight helicopter, indicating the severity of the collision warranted emergency air transport to a hospital. However, authorities have not disclosed the extent of her injuries or provided updates on her current condition.

The incident occurred near Thelma Crenshaw Elementary School, which serves students in grades Pre-K-5 and is part of Chesterfield County Public Schools. The school is located in the Midlothian area of the county and serves approximately 708 students.

Chesterfield County has been focusing on pedestrian and school zone safety in recent months. The county launched a school zone speed enforcement camera program, with cameras monitoring speeding in active school zones during arrival and dismissal hours. The county also held a May 2026 public hearing on a proposed Woods Edge Road at Route 1 pedestrian crossing project.
