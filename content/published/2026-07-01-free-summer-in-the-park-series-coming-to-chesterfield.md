# Free Summer in the Park Series Coming to Chesterfield

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation is hosting a free Summer in the Park series at five county parks in July, featuring music, food, and the chance to meet district supervisors.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation; five district supervisors
- **What:** Free Summer in the Park Series with music, food, and community gathering
- **When:** July 9, 14, 16, 28, and 30, 2026; 5:30-7:30 p.m. each evening
- **Where:** Five county parks: Cogbill, Clarendon, Horner, Harrowgate, and Huguenot

## The story

Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation is bringing free summer entertainment to residents across the county with its Summer in the Park Series, a five-date event series running through July. Each evening, from 5:30 to 7:30 p.m., county residents can enjoy music, food, and outdoor activities at parks throughout the county, with complimentary hot dogs, ice cream, and other vendor options available.

The series rotates through five county parks, each paired with a district supervisor who will be present to meet with residents. The schedule kicks off July 9 at Cogbill Park (6700 Cogbill Road) with Dale District Supervisor Dr. LeQuan Hylton, followed by July 14 at Clarendon Park (2428 Brookforest Road) with Clover Hill District Supervisor Jessica Schneider. The series continues July 16 at Horner Park (17701 Genito Road) with Matoaca District Supervisor Kevin Carroll, July 28 at Harrowgate Park (4001 Cougar Trail) with Bermuda District Supervisor Jim Ingle, and concludes July 30 at Huguenot Park (10901 Robious Road) with Midlothian District Supervisor Dr. Mark Miller.

All programs are free to attend with no registration required, making them accessible to all county families. Residents are invited to play, dance, and enjoy summer activities while connecting with their district supervisor and neighbors. Food and entertainment vendors will be on site at each location.

For more information, residents can contact the Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation department at 804-748-1000 or visit chesterfield.gov. More details are also available through the Parks and Recreation Programs page on the county website.

## The case for

Free community events remove financial barriers to summer entertainment and outdoor recreation, allowing all residents regardless of income to enjoy county amenities and activities. The series provides informal opportunities for residents to meet elected district supervisors and county staff, strengthening community engagement and accessibility to local government.

## The case against

Funding these events through county resources could be viewed as a discretionary use of tax dollars when some residents might prioritize funding for essential services like infrastructure, education, or public safety. Evening events in July may face attendance challenges due to heat and competing summer activities, potentially limiting the cost-effectiveness of the investment across all five locations.

**Why it matters:** The series provides free, accessible entertainment for county residents during summer months while strengthening connection between residents and their elected representatives. For families with limited budgets, these free events offer seasonal recreation and community gathering opportunities throughout the county.
