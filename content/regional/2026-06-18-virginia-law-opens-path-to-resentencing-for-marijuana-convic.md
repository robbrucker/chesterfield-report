# Virginia Law Opens Path to Resentencing for Marijuana Convictions

**TL;DR:** A new Virginia law allows certain people with prior marijuana convictions to request resentencing hearings.

Virginia has passed legislation enabling resentencing hearings for individuals with select marijuana convictions. The law represents an opportunity for people previously convicted under stricter penalties to seek review of their sentences. Details on eligibility and the timeline for requesting hearings will likely depend on the specific conviction and when it occurred.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents with prior marijuana convictions—or their family members—may now have an avenue to request sentence review and potential reduction.
