# Virginia May Delay Higher School Standards for Two Years

**TL;DR:** Virginia is considering pushing back the timeline for when stricter reading and math standards would take effect in schools.

Virginia's Board of Education is considering a two-year delay in implementing higher academic benchmarks for reading and math. Rather than gradually increasing standards over four years starting this year, the state would extend the timeline by two years. The shift comes after criticism over the original implementation plan.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield schools follow Virginia academic standards, so this delay would extend the timeline for when local students would need to meet tougher reading and math benchmarks.
