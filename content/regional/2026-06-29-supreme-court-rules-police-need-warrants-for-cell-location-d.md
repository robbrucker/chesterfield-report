# Supreme Court Rules Police Need Warrants for Cell Location Data

**TL;DR:** The U.S. Supreme Court ruled that police must obtain warrants before searching cellphone location histories in investigations, citing Fourth Amendment protections.

The U.S. Supreme Court determined Monday that law enforcement must get warrants before searching cellphone location records near crime scenes, extending Fourth Amendment protections to digital location data. The ruling applies to all police searches for this type of information. The court left open the question of when such warrants would be legally reasonable, likely signaling future rulings on the specifics.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield County residents now have stronger legal protection against warrantless police searches of their cellphone location history during criminal investigations.
