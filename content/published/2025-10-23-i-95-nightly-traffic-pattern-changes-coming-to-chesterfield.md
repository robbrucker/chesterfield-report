# I-95 nightly traffic pattern changes coming to Chesterfield

**TL;DR:** VDOT announced nightly traffic pattern changes on I-95 in Chesterfield County, including lane closures and slow rolls for bridge and infrastructure work through June 2026.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT)
- **What:** Nightly lane closures, slow rolls, and ramp closures on I-95 for bridge repairs, overpass work, and infrastructure installation
- **When:** February through June 2026, varying nightly hours (typically 11 p.m. to 5 a.m.)
- **Where:** I-95 in Chesterfield County, near Route 10, Route 288, Coxendale Road, and West Hundred Road

## The story

The Virginia Department of Transportation has announced a series of nightly traffic pattern changes along Interstate 95 in Chesterfield County's Richmond District, with work scheduled through June 2026. The changes include lane closures, controlled slow rolls, and ramp closures on both northbound and southbound I-95 during overnight hours to allow crews to conduct essential bridge repairs, overpass maintenance, and infrastructure installation while minimizing daytime traffic disruption.

VDOT began work in February 2026 with slow rolls on southbound I-95 between mile marker 63 (near Route 613/Willis Road) and mile marker 60 (near Route 10/West Hundred Road) from 1 a.m. to 5 a.m., February 9–12, for the raising and lowering of bridge girders over West Hundred Road. More extensive work followed in April, with double lane closures on northbound I-95 between mile marker 60 (Route 10/Exit 61) and mile marker 62 (Route 288/Exit 62) for repairs to the Coxendale Road overpass, scheduled nightly from 11 p.m. to 5 a.m. The Route 288 ramp (Exit 62) closed during these periods, with VDOT providing detour routes.

In May 2026, VDOT scheduled digital message sign structure installations requiring two-lane closures on both northbound and southbound I-95 near the Chesterfield County boundaries with Richmond and Colonial Heights. Work occurred nightly from 9 p.m. to 6 a.m. on Tuesday through Thursday, May 26–28, between mile markers 53 and 57, and again between mile markers 68 and 72. This is part of broader infrastructure modernization to improve traveler information along the I-95 corridor.

All work is weather-dependent and subject to change. VDOT encourages commuters to check real-time traffic updates on 511 Virginia (511.vdot.virginia.gov) or via the free 511 Virginia mobile app before traveling overnight hours. Drivers should expect slowdowns, use caution in work zones, and consider adjusting travel times during affected periods.

- **2026-05-26** — Digital message sign installation work began on I-95 near county boundaries

## The case for

Nightly work schedules minimize daytime traffic congestion and safety hazards for the high-volume I-95 commute corridor. By conducting essential bridge repairs, overpass maintenance, and infrastructure upgrades during off-peak hours, VDOT reduces impact to the hundreds of thousands of daily drivers who depend on I-95 for work and commerce. Modernizing digital message signs improves real-time traveler information and emergency response capabilities throughout the corridor.

## The case against

Overnight lane closures disrupt shift workers, late-night commuters, and truckers who depend on I-95 during overnight hours. Double lane closures on a major interstate can create bottlenecks and force traffic onto local roads, potentially affecting neighborhoods near exit ramps. Extended maintenance windows (months-long schedules) create ongoing unpredictability for residents and businesses, and weather delays can cascade changes throughout the work season.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield County residents and commuters using I-95 overnight should plan for altered travel times, potential delays, and possible reroutes. Understanding the scope and timing of these changes helps drivers adjust schedules, avoid peak closure windows, and use alternate routes when necessary.
