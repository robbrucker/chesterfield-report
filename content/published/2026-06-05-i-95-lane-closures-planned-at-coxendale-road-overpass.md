# I-95 Lane Closures Planned at Coxendale Road Overpass

**TL;DR:** VDOT will close two northbound I-95 lanes nightly at the Coxendale Road overpass in Chesterfield County for structural repairs.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT)
- **What:** Nightly double lane closures on northbound I-95 for overpass repairs
- **When:** Nightly 11 p.m. to 5 a.m., weather permitting (began April 2026)
- **Where:** I-95 northbound between Mile Marker 60 (Route 10/Exit 61) and Mile Marker 62 (Route 288/Exit 62), Chesterfield County

## The story

The Virginia Department of Transportation has begun nightly double lane closures on northbound Interstate 95 in Chesterfield County to repair the Coxendale Road overpass structure. Work occurs between mile marker 60 (Route 10/Exit 61) and mile marker 62 (Route 288/Exit 62), with the northbound Route 288 ramp (Exit 62) also closed during repair operations.

Weather permitting, closures are scheduled from 11 p.m. to 5 a.m. nightly to minimize impact on daytime traffic. This phased nighttime approach allows VDOT to maintain essential corridor flow during peak commute periods while completing necessary structural maintenance on the overpass.

Drivers needing to access northbound Route 288 during closures should use a detour: continue northbound on I-95 to Exit 64, turn left onto Willis Road, access the southbound I-95 ramp, and return to northbound Route 288. VDOT recommends monitoring real-time traffic conditions via 511 Virginia or VDOT's website for updates on work schedules.

The Coxendale Road overpass is a critical crossing point on I-95 in the central Chesterfield area, carrying regional traffic between Richmond and points north. Residents and commuters are urged to allow extra travel time and consider alternate routes during the nightly work windows.

## Key players

- **Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT)** — Agency conducting overpass repair work and managing closures

## Key dates

- **2026-04-24** — VDOT announced nightly closure schedule for Coxendale Road overpass repairs

## The case for

Timely maintenance of the Coxendale Road overpass is essential infrastructure upkeep that prevents more costly structural problems down the road. Completing repairs now protects the integrity of a critical I-95 crossing that serves tens of thousands of daily commuters and regional commerce. Scheduling work during off-peak nighttime hours (11 p.m.–5 a.m.) minimizes disruption to daytime traffic and commutes.

## The case against

Nightly lane closures, even if scheduled after hours, reduce I-95's northbound capacity during a key maintenance window and may create congestion during evening and late-night shifts. Drivers using the Route 288 interchange must navigate a lengthy detour via Willis Road, adding time to trips. Long-term or extended closures could impact commute reliability if work takes longer than expected or weather delays extend the project timeline.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents and regional commuters who use I-95 northbound in this corridor should plan for potential delays and longer travel times, particularly during evening and overnight hours. The work is critical for maintaining a major transportation artery that connects the Richmond area to northern Virginia and beyond.
