# HCA Opens New Emergency Room in Chesterfield

**TL;DR:** HCA Health System opened a new 12,000-square-foot freestanding emergency room at 9630 Iron Bridge Road in Chesterfield County in June 2026, expanding emergency care capacity south of the James River.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** HCA Health System (Chippenham Hospital, CJW Medical Center)
- **What:** Opened freestanding emergency room with 12 treatment rooms, full imaging, 24/7 care
- **When:** June 2026
- **Where:** 9630 Iron Bridge Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

HCA Health System marked a significant expansion of emergency care in Chesterfield County with the opening of a new freestanding emergency room at 9630 Iron Bridge Road in June 2026. The 12,000-square-foot facility, operated as a campus of CJW Medical Center and affiliated with Chippenham Hospital, features 12 emergency treatment rooms and comprehensive imaging capabilities including CT scan and X-ray services. The center provides around-the-clock emergency care and serves as a dedicated emergency facility distinct from a traditional hospital-based ER.

The Chesterfield location represents a key component of HCA Virginia's broader emergency care expansion strategy across Central and Northern Virginia. The system is investing nearly $60 million to grow its freestanding emergency room footprint from 8 to 11 locations by 2026. Beyond the Chesterfield site, HCA is opening additional freestanding ERs in Richmond and Loudoun County, reflecting the health system's commitment to expanding emergency care capacity in rapidly growing communities across Virginia.

State regulators approved HCA's plan for the facility as part of a competitive landscape where multiple healthcare systems are seeking to enhance emergency care access in Chesterfield County. The approval came as Virginia's Department of Health evaluated various hospital expansion proposals from HCA, VCU Health, and Bon Secours Health System, each proposing substantial capital investments to address growing healthcare needs in the region.

The freestanding ER model allows HCA to extend emergency care services beyond its traditional hospital campuses, reducing wait times and providing rapid access to emergency services for county residents. The facility complements HCA's existing emergency care presence, including Chippenham Hospital in Richmond and Johnston-Willis Hospital south of the James River.

## The case for

Freestanding ERs increase emergency care access and reduce wait times by adding dedicated emergency capacity without requiring a full hospital facility. For a growing county like Chesterfield, the model provides cost-efficient emergency services at convenient locations and allows residents rapid access to imaging, labs, and emergency stabilization without traveling to distant hospital campuses.

## The case against

Freestanding ERs may fragment emergency care delivery and create challenges for patients requiring admission to inpatient beds, who must be transferred to a full hospital. Some healthcare experts worry that competing freestanding facilities from multiple systems can duplicate services and raise overall regional costs rather than create genuine new capacity, particularly when traditional hospitals already serve the area.

**Why it matters:** The new ER directly improves emergency care access for Chesterfield residents by providing immediate, local emergency services with no transport delay. As the county's population and healthcare demand grow, the facility addresses capacity constraints and provides an alternative to traveling to Richmond-based hospitals during emergencies.
