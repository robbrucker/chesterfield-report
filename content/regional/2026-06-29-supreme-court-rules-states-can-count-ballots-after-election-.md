# Supreme Court rules states can count ballots after Election Day

**TL;DR:** The U.S. Supreme Court said states can count mail-in ballots that arrive after Election Day.

In a 5-4 decision, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled Monday that federal law does not prevent states from counting mail-in ballots that arrive after Election Day. The Trump administration and some Republican states had argued that all ballots should arrive by the close of polls, but the Court rejected that position. The decision affirms states' authority to set their own rules for late-arriving mail-in ballots.

**Why it matters:** The ruling allows Chesterfield County election officials to count mail-in ballots that arrive after Election Day, potentially including more votes in final election tallies.
