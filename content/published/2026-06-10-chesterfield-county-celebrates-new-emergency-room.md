# Chesterfield County Celebrates New Emergency Room

**TL;DR:** HCA Virginia opened a $16.4 million freestanding emergency room in Chesterfield County on June 10, 2026, marking the expansion of emergency medical services for the region's fast-growing population.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** HCA Virginia (Chippenham Hospital), local officials and community leaders
- **What:** Ribbon-cutting ceremony and opening of new 24-hour freestanding emergency room
- **When:** June 10, 2026
- **Where:** 9630 Iron Bridge Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

HCA Virginia celebrated the opening of the Chesterfield Emergency Room on June 10, 2026, with a ribbon-cutting ceremony marking a major expansion of emergency medical services in the county. The new facility, located at 9630 Iron Bridge Road, is a 12,000-square-foot freestanding emergency room that operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year with board-certified emergency physicians and nurses providing the same level of care available in a hospital-based emergency department.

The facility features 12 treatment rooms and comprehensive diagnostic imaging including CT and X-ray capabilities, allowing it to treat a full range of acute conditions from heart attacks and strokes to trauma, burns, and overdoses. The $16.4 million investment was completed by general contractor Hitt Construction and designed by HD Architects. The new ER operates as part of the CJW Medical Center campus and an arm of HCA's Chippenham Hospital.

The opening reflects HCA Virginia's broader regional investment strategy, part of a $60 million initiative to expand emergency and urgent care access across Virginia. The Chesterfield ER is one of three new freestanding emergency rooms HCA opened in 2026—joining the Scott's Walk ER in Richmond (also opened in June) and the Leesburg ER (opened in September). This expansion grew HCA Virginia's freestanding ER footprint from 8 to 11 locations statewide.

The new facility directly responds to Chesterfield County's rapid population growth. The county's estimated 403,000 residents reflect a 10% increase since the 2020 census, with state projections forecasting another 42,000 residents by 2030. This growth has prompted Virginia to approve three major hospital projects totaling $672 million in new capacity—including HCA's planned Magnolia Hospital in Moseley, a new VCU Health hospital, and Bon Secours' expansion of St. Francis Medical Center—positioning Chesterfield for significantly expanded regional healthcare infrastructure in coming years.

## The case for

The new emergency room directly addresses demonstrated demand from rapid population growth, reducing wait times and improving access to urgent care for Chesterfield residents. A dedicated freestanding facility shortens transport times for emergency patients who might otherwise travel to hospital-based emergency departments, potentially improving outcomes for time-sensitive conditions like heart attacks and strokes. The facility also generates local employment and demonstrates continued healthcare investment in the county, supporting long-term economic development.

## The case against

HCA's expansion strategy prioritizes freestanding emergency rooms over full-service hospitals, which some argue provides less comprehensive emergency care than hospital-integrated facilities. Concerns exist about sustainability of multiple competing emergency rooms—if Chesterfield eventually adds two more major hospitals as approved, the region may face over-capacity or inefficient resource allocation. Additionally, freestanding ERs are generally profitable but less accountable to Certificate of Public Need oversight than hospital-based departments, raising questions about equity in which communities receive which types of emergency infrastructure.

**Why it matters:** The facility directly serves Chesterfield residents by reducing emergency response times and improving access to critical care during medical emergencies. The opening also signals confidence in the county's growth trajectory and positions Chesterfield to handle healthcare demand while it welcomes nearly 43,000 additional residents projected by 2030.
