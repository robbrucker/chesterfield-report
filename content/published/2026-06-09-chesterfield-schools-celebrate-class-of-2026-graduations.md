# Chesterfield Schools Celebrate Class of 2026 Graduations

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County Public Schools celebrated the Class of 2026 with graduation ceremonies held across ten high schools in May 2026, with livestreamed access allowing families to witness the milestone despite capacity limits.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Class of 2026 graduating seniors from Chesterfield County Public Schools
- **What:** High school graduation ceremonies honoring student achievement and transitions to next chapter
- **When:** May 18–22 and May 27, 2026
- **Where:** Virginia State University Multipurpose Center, Virginia Commonwealth University Siegel Center, and Thomas R. Fulghum Conference Center

## The story

Chesterfield County Public Schools marked a significant milestone in May 2026 as the Class of 2026 celebrated graduation across ten high schools. The ceremonies were held May 18–22 at two main venues—Virginia State University's Multipurpose Center and Virginia Commonwealth University's Siegel Center—with an additional ceremony for Carver College and Career Academy on May 27 at the Thomas R. Fulghum Conference Center.

The school district served 62,673 students across 73 schools in 2026, with the graduating class representing the culmination of students' K–12 educational journeys in Chesterfield. Dr. John Murray, superintendent since January 2025, oversaw the celebrations; notably, Murray was himself a Chesterfield County public school alumnus, having attended Chalkley Elementary, Jacobs Road Elementary, and Manchester Middle before graduating from Manchester High School.

While in-person attendance was limited to graduates and staff, Chesterfield County Public Schools livestreamed all ceremonies on the district's YouTube channel, with recordings available afterward. This approach allowed families and community members to witness the ceremonies regardless of physical access constraints to the venues. The timing of graduations spanned five days, with most ceremonies scheduled in morning and afternoon slots to accommodate all ten schools.

The Class of 2026 represented part of a district serving a diverse student body: 42.9 percent white, 26.3 percent Black, 21.5 percent Hispanic/Latino, 3.3 percent Asian or Asian/Pacific Islander, and smaller percentages of other backgrounds; 35.7 percent of the district's students are economically disadvantaged. Recent enrollment trends show Chesterfield's K–12 public school enrollment has plateaued slightly, with enrollment dipping by 131 students from 2022–25 despite the county's overall population growing by more than 15,000 residents.

## The case for

Livestreaming graduations extends access beyond capacity-limited in-person venues, allowing families unable to secure tickets or attend in person—including those with work conflicts, mobility challenges, or distance—to witness this important milestone without missing the moment.

## The case against

Restricting in-person attendance limits the traditional graduation experience of an open celebration where extended family, neighbors, and community members can gather to honor graduates; livestreaming, while valuable, cannot fully replicate the shared in-person atmosphere and emotional resonance of a public ceremony.

**Why it matters:** For Chesterfield County families, graduation represents the culmination of years of public education in a district serving nearly 63,000 students. The Class of 2026's transition to college, careers, and adulthood reflects the district's role in preparing the next generation of county residents and contributors.
