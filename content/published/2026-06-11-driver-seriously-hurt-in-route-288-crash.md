# Driver Seriously Hurt in Route 288 Crash

**TL;DR:** A driver sustained serious but non-life-threatening injuries when their vehicle overturned on Route 288 north of Courthouse Road on Thursday afternoon, June 11.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** One driver
- **What:** Single-vehicle crash and overturn on Route 288
- **When:** Thursday, June 11, 2026 at 12:24 p.m.
- **Where:** Route 288 north of Courthouse Road exit, Chesterfield County

## The story

A driver was seriously injured Thursday afternoon when their vehicle overturned on Route 288 in Chesterfield County. The single-vehicle crash occurred at 12:24 p.m. north of the Courthouse Road exit, with the vehicle coming to rest on its side. Virginia State Police responded and transported the driver to a nearby hospital with serious but non-life-threatening injuries.

The crash temporarily closed the southbound right lane and right shoulder of Route 288, affecting traffic flow through the busy interchange corridor. The cause of the crash remains under investigation by state police, and no additional details about the vehicle or driver have been disclosed.

This incident highlights the traffic challenges at the Route 288 and Courthouse Road interchange, one of Chesterfield County's highest-volume transportation corridors. The area handles approximately 58,000 vehicles per day on Route 288 and 57,000 on Iron Bridge Road (Route 10), with ongoing county mitigation projects aimed at improving safety through the weave sections of the interchange.

**Why it matters:** The crash underscores safety concerns on Route 288, a major county corridor that has seen multiple serious accidents. County traffic engineering efforts focus on this high-volume interchange to reduce crashes and improve traffic flow.
