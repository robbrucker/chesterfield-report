# Police Department Running 'Christmas in July' Toy Drive

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County Police is running a "Christmas in July" toy drive through July 31, 2026, collecting new and unwrapped toys at five department locations for distribution to local children in December.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Police Department, Chester Food Pantry
- **What:** Christmas in July toy drive collecting new, unwrapped toys for children ages 0-12
- **When:** July 1-31, 2026; distribution in December
- **Where:** Five Chesterfield County Police stations (Headquarters, Appomattox, Falling Creek, Falling Creek Substation, Swift Creek stations)

## The story

The Chesterfield County Police Department has launched its annual "Christmas in July" toy drive, running through July 31, 2026. The initiative invites residents to donate new, unwrapped toys for children ages 0-12 as a way to bring joy to local families during the summer season. All collected items will be stored and then distributed in December by the Chester Food Pantry, extending the holiday spirit to children in need throughout the community.

Residents can drop off toys at five convenient locations across Chesterfield County. The department's main headquarters is located at 10001 Iron Bridge Road, while satellite drop-off points include the Appomattox Station (2920 W. Hundred Road), Falling Creek Station (20 N. Providence Road), Falling Creek Substation (5701 Route 1), and Swift Creek Station (6812 Woodlake Commons Loop). This distributed approach makes it easier for community members throughout the county to participate.

The toy drive reflects a broader community engagement mission for law enforcement, allowing police officers to connect with residents in a positive, charitable context. By partnering with the Chester Food Pantry for December distribution, the program ensures toys reach families who can benefit most from the holiday gifts. The annual initiative has become a staple of the department's community outreach efforts.

For those interested in participating, the Chesterfield County Police Department encourages donors to ensure toys are new and unwrapped. More details are available through the department's official social media channels and website.

## Key players

- **Chesterfield County Police Department:** Organizer and collector of toy donations
- **Chester Food Pantry:** Partner distributing toys to children in December

## Key dates

- **2026-07-01:** Christmas in July toy drive begins
- **2026-07-31:** Christmas in July toy drive ends
- **2026-12:** Collected toys distributed by Chester Food Pantry

## The case for

Community toy drives like this one strengthen relationships between police and residents while directly supporting local families during the holiday season. The summer collection period allows time for proper storage and organization before December distribution, and the partnership with Chester Food Pantry ensures efficient delivery to families in need. Police participation in charitable giving improves community trust and morale.

## The case against

While toy drives serve a benevolent purpose, some community members raise questions about whether police departments should focus charitable resources on areas outside public safety. Residents may wonder if the same volunteer effort and department resources could address year-round food insecurity or child welfare needs beyond a single holiday season. Additionally, toys collected in summer for December distribution may require extended storage space and oversight.

**Why it matters:** This toy drive directly impacts vulnerable children in Chesterfield County by ensuring families facing economic hardship can still provide holiday gifts. The initiative also builds positive relationships between law enforcement and the community, demonstrating police commitment to local welfare beyond enforcement.
