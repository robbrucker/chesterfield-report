# Search Warrants Reveal Details of Chesterfield Officer Shooting

**TL;DR:** Recently unsealed search warrants from a May 31 officer-involved shooting in Chesterfield County reveal investigators seized weapons, ammunition, body armor, and tactical gear from the suspect's home.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Gary Gene Shaw, 65; Officers Jacob Clark and Jacob Wells; K-9 Knight
- **What:** Officer-involved shooting during domestic disturbance call; two officers shot, K-9 killed; search warrants reveal weapons and tactical equipment seized
- **When:** May 31, 2026 (incident); June 11, 2026 (search warrant details released)
- **Where:** 12900 block of Gumfork Place, Chesterfield County, Virginia

## The story

On May 31, 2026, at approximately 4:40 p.m., Chesterfield County Police responded to a domestic disturbance call at a home in the 12900 block of Gumfork Place. A woman reported that a man inside the residence had fired shots during a family dispute and was attempting to force his way into an upstairs bedroom where she had taken shelter. When uniformed officers entered the home, the suspect opened fire with a long gun, striking two officers and a police canine in an exchange of gunfire.

Officers Jacob Clark, 25, and Jacob Wells, 40, both members of the Swift Creek Patrol Division, were critically injured. Clark sustained seven gunshot wounds, while Wells was shot twice. Both officers remained hospitalized in critical but stable condition following the incident. K-9 Knight, a 5-year-old German Shepherd and Malinois mix who had served with the department for four years, was fatally wounded and died in the line of duty. The suspect, Gary Gene Shaw, 65, was also shot and hospitalized under police custody.

Recently unsealed search warrants obtained on June 1 and executed on June 2 provide details into the scope of investigators' examination of Shaw's home and the evidence collected. The warrants reveal that police seized an extensive arsenal and tactical equipment, including thousands of various cartridges and ammunition, AK-47 rifle parts and conversion manuals, body armor plates, muskets, rifles, and grenades with black electrical tape. Officers also recovered AR-15 and AK-style weapon parts, bags of miscellaneous components, and military-style equipment. The breadth of weapons and materials seized underscores the nature of the investigation into Shaw's preparation and intent.

Shaw has been charged with 17 felony counts related to the incident, including two counts of attempted aggravated murder of a law enforcement officer, two counts of aggravated malicious wounding of a police officer, and additional charges brought by prosecutors in June as the investigation progressed. The community rallied to support the injured officers, with blood drives, fundraisers, and a growing memorial for K-9 Knight at the Chesterfield Police Building on Iron Bridge Road. A local restaurant dedicated its dog-friendly patio to Knight's memory and planned to install a bronze plaque honoring his service.
