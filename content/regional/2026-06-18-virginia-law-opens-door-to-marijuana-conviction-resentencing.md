# Virginia Law Opens Door to Marijuana Conviction Resentencing

**TL;DR:** Virginia has enacted a law that automatically triggers resentencing hearings for people with certain marijuana convictions on their records.

A new Virginia law creates an automatic process to reopen resentencing hearings for individuals with some marijuana convictions. The law allows eligible people the opportunity to have their cases reconsidered. This reflects evolving state policy around marijuana-related offenses.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents with prior marijuana convictions may now qualify for resentencing review, potentially affecting their records and futures.
