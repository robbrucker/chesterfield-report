# Iron Bridge Road in Chester reopens after power line repairs

**TL;DR:** Iron Bridge Road in Chester reopened after VDOT completed repairs to downed power lines that temporarily shut down the route.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** VDOT crews, Dominion Energy
- **What:** Downed power lines forced temporary closure of Iron Bridge Road (Route 10); all lanes reopened after repairs
- **When:** June 17, 2026
- **Where:** Iron Bridge Road (Route 10) near Chalkley Road in Chester, Chesterfield County

## The story

Downed power lines forced the closure of Iron Bridge Road (Route 10) in Chester on June 17, 2026, disrupting traffic near the Chalkley Road area. The Virginia Department of Transportation directed the temporary shutdown while crews worked to repair the damaged utility lines serving the area.

Iron Bridge Road is a primary state route through Chesterfield County's Chester district. The thoroughfare carries significant daily traffic and serves as a key connector in the county's transportation network. The road study underway for Iron Bridge Road (the STARS study) is examining multi-modal transportation improvements along the corridor.

Dominion Energy crews and VDOT worked to complete repairs to the downed lines during the closure. By the end of the day, all lanes of Iron Bridge Road had reopened to traffic. No injuries were reported in connection with the incident.

Motorists during the closure were directed to use alternative routes or follow posted detour signs. VDOT advised drivers to check real-time traffic conditions through Virginia's 511 system for any ongoing impacts.

## Key players

- **Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT):** Directed road closure and coordinated repair operations
- **Dominion Energy:** Utility company responsible for power line infrastructure and repairs

## The case for

A rapid response and repairs restoration maintains critical transportation infrastructure and minimizes disruption to commuters and local businesses that depend on Iron Bridge Road for access.

## The case against

Incidents like downed power lines raise questions about the condition and maintenance of utility infrastructure in the area and whether aging infrastructure needs proactive replacement or upgrades to prevent future disruptions.

**Why it matters:** Iron Bridge Road is a major thoroughfare in Chester serving thousands of daily commuters and providing access to area businesses and neighborhoods; temporary closures create traffic bottlenecks and affect economic activity until repairs are complete.
