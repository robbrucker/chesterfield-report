# Couple Displaced After House Fire in Chesterfield

**TL;DR:** A couple escaped safely after a house fire at their home on Millers Run Road in Chesterfield County on Saturday evening, June 27, with both residents being displaced but uninjured.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Two residents of a home in Chesterfield County
- **What:** House fire with heavy smoke and flames reported at 8:53 p.m.
- **When:** Saturday, June 27, 2026, at 8:53 p.m.
- **Where:** 9900 block of Millers Run Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

Chesterfield County Fire and EMS responded to a residential structure fire at 9900 Millers Run Road on Saturday, June 27, 2026, at 8:53 p.m. Upon arrival, firefighters found heavy smoke coming from the back of the home and called additional units to assist with suppression efforts. Fire personnel worked to contain the blaze, and the fire was marked under control in under an hour and a half.

The two residents who were inside the home at the time of the fire evacuated safely without sustaining injuries. Officials confirmed that both residents are now displaced from their home as a result of the fire. According to fire officials, the couple is receiving assistance from family members during their temporary relocation. The cause of the fire remains under investigation by Chesterfield County Fire Services.

Chesterfield County Fire and EMS operates 23 strategically located fire stations staffed by career firefighters and emergency medical personnel. The department responds to all fire emergencies including structure fires, hazardous materials incidents, and rescue operations. For residents displaced by fire or other emergencies, Chesterfield County offers various support resources through its Department of Social Services, including emergency housing assistance programs and financial aid through organizations like Homeward's Homeless Connection Line.

## Key players
