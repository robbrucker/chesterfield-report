# Dump truck overturned, hazmat crew responds

**TL;DR:** A dump truck overturned on Bradley Bridge Road in Chesterfield County on June 5, 2026, prompting hazmat crews to respond to a fuel leak.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield Fire and EMS, hazmat team, dump truck driver
- **What:** Dump truck overturned with fuel leak requiring hazmat containment
- **When:** June 5, 2026
- **Where:** Bradley Bridge Road near Lewis Road, Chesterfield County, Virginia

## The story

An overturned dump truck closed Bradley Bridge Road near Lewis Road in Chesterfield County on June 5, 2026, drawing emergency crews to the scene to contain a fuel leak. The driver was injured in the crash, though officials did not confirm the severity of injuries. Chesterfield Fire and EMS dispatched hazmat team members to the location to manage the spill and prevent environmental contamination.

Road closures forced drivers to seek alternate routes while crews cleaned up the scene. The hazmat team's presence indicated the truck's cargo required specialized handling procedures beyond standard towing and debris removal. Fuel spills from heavy trucks present risks to groundwater and local waterways, making containment a priority for emergency responders.

Bradley Bridge Road serves as a key connector in the county's road network. Chesterfield Fire and EMS's hazmat specialists are trained to handle chemical spills, fuel leaks, and other hazardous material incidents that require containment and environmental protection measures.
