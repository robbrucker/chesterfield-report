# Longtime Administrator Joe Casey Announces Retirement

**TL;DR:** Dr. Joseph P. Casey, Chesterfield County administrator for a decade, announced his retirement effective July 1, 2026, prompting a leadership search by the Board of Supervisors.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Dr. Joseph P. Casey
- **What:** County Administrator announces retirement effective July 1, 2026, after 10 years overseeing Virginia's fourth-largest county
- **When:** Announcement December 2025; retirement July 1, 2026
- **Where:** Chesterfield County, Virginia

## The story

Dr. Joseph P. Casey announced his retirement as Chesterfield County Administrator effective July 1, 2026—exactly ten years after taking the position in 2016. The announcement came in December 2025, giving the Board of Supervisors ample time to conduct a comprehensive search for his successor. Casey cited personal reasons for stepping down, including his desire to spend more time with his family and serve as a caretaker for his aging mother.

Casey's decade at the helm of Virginia's fourth-largest county has been marked by significant economic development and infrastructure investment. Under his leadership, major corporations including LEGO, Google, and Commonwealth Fusion Systems established operations or announced plans in Chesterfield, representing $12 billion in capital investment and the creation of 10,200 jobs. The county also invested more than $1 billion in public facilities during his tenure, including 12 new schools and three fire stations.

Before joining Chesterfield in 2016, Casey spent 25 years in public administration in the Richmond region. He began his career as a deputy county administrator in Hanover County in 1990, later held the same position in Henrico County, and brought a track record of fiscal management to Chesterfield. Under his oversight, the county achieved quadruple-AAA bond ratings from four agencies, reflecting strong financial stewardship and investor confidence.

The county contracted with Raftelis, a consulting firm, to conduct the administrator search. As of May 2026, approximately 40 candidates from across the United States had applied for the position. Casey's retirement comes amid broader leadership transitions at the county: Economic Development Director Garrett Hart announced his retirement in early 2026, and Planning Director Andy Gillies also planned to step down in the coming months.

- **Dr. Joseph P. Casey** — Chesterfield County Administrator (2016–2026)
- **Chesterfield County Board of Supervisors** — Responsible for recruiting Casey's successor
- **Raftelis** — Search firm contracted to assist in recruitment

## Key dates

- **2026-07-01** — Joe Casey retirement effective date
- **2026-06-30** — Deadline for successor to assume office before Casey's departure

## The case for

Casey's retirement creates an opportunity for the county to bring in fresh leadership with potentially different priorities or approaches. The recruitment process allows the Board of Supervisors to evaluate candidates who may bring new perspectives on economic development, service delivery, or county operations. Additionally, succession planning conducted well in advance reduces the risk of operational disruption and allows time for transition planning.

## The case against

Losing a long-tenured administrator after a decade of successful leadership—including major economic development wins and strong fiscal management—means the county loses experienced institutional knowledge and established relationships with major corporate partners and regional stakeholders. The timing of multiple retirements (administrator, economic development director, planning director) within months creates compounded turnover risk and may disrupt continuity in ongoing projects and initiatives.

**Why it matters:** Casey's departure marks a significant leadership change for Chesterfield residents and the county's business community. The new administrator will inherit a county of approximately 340,000 residents with a $2.4 billion budget and responsibility for 4,000 employees. The leadership transition will shape the county's direction on economic development priorities, service provision, and long-term planning—making the quality of the recruitment process and successor's vision directly relevant to residents' quality of life and the county's fiscal health.
