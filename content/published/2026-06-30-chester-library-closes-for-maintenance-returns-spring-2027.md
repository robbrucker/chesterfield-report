# Chester Library Closes for Maintenance, Returns Spring 2027

**TL;DR:** Chester Library closes July 1 for an eight-month facility maintenance project including a new roof, HVAC system, and interior upgrades, with spring 2027 reopening expected.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Public Library system and county residents
- **What:** Chester Library closure for major facility maintenance including roof, HVAC, and interior upgrades
- **When:** Closes July 1, 2026, reopens Spring 2027
- **Where:** Chester Library, 11800 Centre Street, Chester

## The story

Chester Library will close its doors starting July 1, 2026, to undergo comprehensive facility maintenance that the county describes as "a new roof, a new HVAC system, and interior upgrades, among other" improvements. The closure is expected to last approximately eight months, with reopening anticipated in Spring 2027. The library has occupied its current location at 11800 Centre Street since 2001, when it relocated from its original Harrowgate Road site that had operated since 1935.

According to Chesterfield County Supervisor Jim Ingle, the county decided to "tackle the building's facility maintenance needs collectively as a way of performing the work in the most efficient manner possible and to avoid having to close the facility multiple times." This consolidation strategy allows the county to complete multiple necessary projects in a single closure rather than scheduling separate shutdowns over an extended period.

Chesterfield Public Library operates 10 branches across the county, serving over 1 million customer visits annually. To minimize disruption, the county has arranged alternative services during Chester Library's closure. Baby Story Time and Pre-K Story Time programs will relocate to the nearby Perkinson Center for the Arts and Education. A pop-up library service will operate at the Chester Farmers Market one Saturday per month starting in August. Patrons can access book holds through other library locations or the Chester Library book locker, which will remain available for most of the construction period. Digital services including e-books, audiobooks, and research databases remain accessible online during the closure.

Chester Library is known for its distinctive architecture designed to reflect the area's colonial heritage, with its brick turret and façade located beside the Chester Village Green. The library also features the Chesterfield Public Library system's largest collection of materials focused on the arts and hosts monthly exhibits of paintings, prints, and photographs by local artists.

## Key players

- **Chesterfield County Public Library:** Operating the library system and coordinating closure and alternative services
- **Jim Ingle:** County Supervisor overseeing the decision to perform facility maintenance efficiently

## Key dates

- **2026-07-01:** Chester Library closes for maintenance
- **2026-08-01:** Pop-up library at Chester Farmers Market begins (monthly Saturday operations, dates to be announced)
- **2027-03-01:** Expected reopening of Chester Library in Spring 2027

## The case for

Consolidating multiple maintenance projects into a single closure is more efficient than scheduling separate shutdowns over months or years, reducing construction-related disruption and allowing the county to complete necessary work while minimizing overall impact to patrons. A fully renovated facility with new roof, HVAC, and interior improvements will better serve the community and extend the library's useful life.

## The case against

An eight-month closure removes direct access to library services for Chester residents and nearby patrons who rely on the facility, particularly for programs, in-person research help, and community meeting spaces. While alternative services are available at other branches and online, the Chester location's unique arts collections and local history resources are less accessible, and some residents may face longer travel distances to alternative branches.

**Why it matters:** Chester Library residents and frequent patrons will need to adjust their routines and travel to alternative library locations for services through spring 2027, while the county's proactive maintenance approach aims to prevent future emergencies or more disruptive repairs at a central community facility that serves the arts, local history, and programming needs of the Chester area.
