# Virginia law allows resentencing hearings for some marijuana convictions

**TL;DR:** A new Virginia law permits people with certain marijuana convictions to request new sentencing hearings.

Virginia has enacted a law creating a pathway for individuals with specific marijuana convictions to petition for resentencing hearings. The change applies to select marijuana-related offenses and allows courts to reconsider sentences in light of current policy. The shift reflects evolving approaches to marijuana convictions across the state.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents with prior marijuana convictions may now have an opportunity to seek reduced sentences or modified terms under the new law.
