# Chesterfield reviews Marlbank Farms neighborhood development

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County is reviewing a proposal to develop 309 homes on farmland near Matoaca Middle School, with community meetings addressing resident concerns about density and rural character.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Emerson Companies and applicant Jeff Geiger
- **What:** Proposal to rezone 200 acres to develop 309-lot Marlbank Farms residential neighborhood
- **When:** Under review as of June 2026
- **Where:** 5700 Hickory Road, near Matoaca Middle School, Matoaca District

## The story

Emerson Companies has filed rezoning plans for Marlbank Farms, a 309-lot residential development on approximately 200 acres in southern Chesterfield near Matoaca Middle School. The project proposes Semi-Urban Residential (SU) zoning with two distinct sections: smaller 55-foot-wide lots near Hickory Road designed for one- and two-level homes, and 32 larger estate lots of one acre within the interior, separated by common areas and greenspace. A stub road would connect to potential future development eastward on the property.

The Matoaca area, known for its rural and semi-rural character, has experienced increasing development pressure. Community meetings revealed that while residents are not categorically opposed to new construction, concerns focus on the density and scale of Marlbank Farms relative to the neighborhood's existing low-density, tree-filled properties. Developers acknowledged these concerns after speaking with nearby homeowners who expressed discomfort with the proposed concentration of units.

Chesterfield County has held multiple public meetings to gather feedback on the rezoning case. Matoaca District Supervisor Kevin Carroll hosted town halls on January 27 and February 11, 2026, at Matoaca Elementary and Middle Schools respectively, and a rescheduled meeting was planned for June 11, 2026, at Matoaca Middle School. County planning and transportation staff have attended these sessions to answer questions and hear resident input.

If approved, Emerson indicated that construction would not begin for at least two years, allowing time for site preparation and home design work. The proposal represents the ongoing tension in Chesterfield between accommodating regional growth and preserving neighborhood character in residential areas.

## The case for

Chesterfield County faces sustained population growth and housing demand; the Marlbank Farms rezoning allows the county to accommodate new residents while planning for future development connections on adjacent land, generating tax revenue and creating housing options across price points through varied lot sizes (ranging from smaller urban-style lots to one-acre estate parcels).

## The case against

The concentration of over 300 homes on a single site represents a significant density increase for a historically rural area, with potential impacts on traffic flow on Hickory Road (already near a middle school), property values and neighborhood character for existing residents, and the adequacy of schools and services in the district if multiple large developments proceed in sequence.

**Why it matters:** The Marlbank Farms decision will shape the future character of the Matoaca area and set precedent for how Chesterfield balances growth demands with community preferences for lower-density development. Residents' concerns about traffic, schools, and quality of life will influence county planning for years to come.
