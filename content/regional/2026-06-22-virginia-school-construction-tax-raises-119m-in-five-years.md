# Virginia School Construction Tax Raises $119M in Five Years

**TL;DR:** Virginia localities have collected $119 million from a sales tax since 2021 to repair aging school buildings, but state data suggests the funding remains insufficient for the scope of needed upgrades.

Virginia established a targeted sales tax in 2021 to fund school construction and maintenance across the state's localities. Over the past five years, that tax has generated $119 million total—but a state survey found more than half of Virginia's schools are older than 50 years. Local officials are now pushing for the tax to be expanded statewide to close the widening infrastructure gap.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents depend on adequate funding for school facility upgrades; the state funding model's effectiveness will determine whether local districts can address aging infrastructure without increased property taxes or deferred maintenance.
