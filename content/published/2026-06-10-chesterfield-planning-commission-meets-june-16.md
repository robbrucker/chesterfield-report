# Chesterfield Planning Commission Meets June 16

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County Planning Commission will hold a public meeting on June 16 where residents can submit comments online, by phone, or in person on cases and development proposals.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Planning Commission
- **What:** Public hearing on planning cases and development proposals
- **When:** Tuesday, June 16, 2026 at 6 p.m.
- **Where:** Public Meeting Room, 10001 Iron Bridge Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

The Chesterfield County Planning Commission will convene on Tuesday, June 16, 2026, at 6 p.m. in the Public Meeting Room at 10001 Iron Bridge Road to hear cases and development proposals. The meeting will include public hearings where residents can comment on rezoning requests, conditional use permits, and other planning matters before the commission.

Residents have multiple options to participate. Those attending in person can sign up in the hallway before the meeting or approach the podium when their agenda item is opened. Seating may be limited, so advance sign-up is encouraged. The meeting will be broadcast on Chesterfield Community Television (WCCT, Comcast channel 98 and Verizon channel 28) and livestreamed on YouTube.com/ChesterfieldCountyVA, allowing residents without access to transportation or those with scheduling conflicts to follow the proceedings.

For those unable to attend, the county offers multiple ways to submit comments. Residents can use an online form at www.chesterfield.gov/cpccomments, email planning@chesterfield.gov, or call 804-748-1125. Comments must be submitted by 5 p.m. on Monday, June 15. The county notes that residents may submit one comment per person per hearing through the online form.

Detailed agendas, staff reports, and PowerPoint presentations for the cases being heard are available at www.chesterfield.gov/plan and through the county's CivicClerk portal. Residents interested in a specific case are encouraged to review these materials before the meeting to better understand the proposals and staff recommendations.

- **2026-06-15** — Deadline for submitting public comments on agenda items
- **2026-06-16** — Planning Commission public hearing meeting at 6 p.m.

## The case for

Remote and in-person participation options increase accessibility for working residents, families with scheduling constraints, and those with transportation limitations. Providing multiple comment channels—online, email, and phone—removes barriers to engagement and ensures a broader cross-section of the community can weigh in on planning decisions. Broadcasting meetings on local cable and YouTube extends reach beyond those able to attend or participate in real time.

## The case against

Limited seating in person may require some residents to choose remote participation even if they prefer face-to-face engagement. The June 15 comment deadline leaves only one business day for residents who learn about a case at the last minute to research and submit remarks. Evening meetings, while common for planning bodies, may still be difficult for shift workers or those with childcare constraints.

**Why it matters:** Planning Commission meetings are where development proposals, rezoning requests, and land-use decisions that shape the county's future are publicly debated and decided. Residents who care about growth, traffic, schools, and neighborhood character in Chesterfield have a direct opportunity to make their voices heard on projects that affect property values, quality of life, and community character.
