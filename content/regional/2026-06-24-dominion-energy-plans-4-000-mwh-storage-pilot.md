# Dominion Energy Plans 4,000 MWh Storage Pilot

**TL;DR:** Dominion Energy Virginia is launching a long-duration energy storage pilot program to help stabilize the power grid.

Dominion Energy Virginia has announced plans for a pilot program testing long-duration energy storage (LDES) technologies with a capacity of 4,000 megawatt-hours. The initiative is part of the utility's efforts to integrate more renewable energy and improve grid reliability. This type of technology can store electricity for extended periods, helping balance supply and demand.

**Why it matters:** Energy storage projects like this one support grid stability and can help manage electricity costs for Chesterfield residents as Virginia transitions toward cleaner energy sources.
