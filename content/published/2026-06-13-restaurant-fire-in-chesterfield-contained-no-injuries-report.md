# Restaurant fire in Chesterfield contained, no injuries reported

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County firefighters contained a cooking-related grease fire at Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant on Hull Street Road in less than 20 minutes with no injuries reported.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Fire and EMS; Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant
- **What:** Cooking-related grease fire at a restaurant
- **When:** Saturday, June 13, 2026, around 9:35 a.m.
- **Where:** Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant, 11100 block of Hull Street Road, Midlothian

## The story

Firefighters responded to a structure fire at Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant on Hull Street Road in Midlothian shortly after 9:30 a.m. on Saturday, June 13. Upon arrival, crews observed smoke emanating from the roof of the building and discovered an active fire inside the restaurant. Acting quickly, firefighters brought the blaze under control in less than 20 minutes.

Fire officials determined the fire was cooking-related, specifically a grease fire that originated in the kitchen area. The quick response and containment efforts prevented the fire from spreading to neighboring businesses in the shopping center. Damage was limited to the restaurant itself, with no impact to adjacent commercial properties.

No injuries were reported from the incident. The restaurant was operating at the time of the fire, though the rapid response by Chesterfield County Fire and EMS ensured the safety of all occupants.

## Key players

- **Chesterfield County Fire and EMS:** Emergency response agency

## The case for

Quick, effective firefighter response contained a potentially dangerous kitchen fire before it could spread, protecting neighboring businesses and demonstrating the value of well-trained emergency services in strip centers with multiple tenants.

## The case against

Kitchen grease fires are preventable through proper maintenance of cooking equipment and staff training; this incident highlights the need for restaurants to ensure their hood systems and suppression equipment are regularly inspected and functional.

**Why it matters:** The incident underscores both the importance of restaurant kitchen safety protocols and the critical role of local fire departments in protecting commercial districts and adjacent businesses from fire spread in mixed-use shopping centers.
