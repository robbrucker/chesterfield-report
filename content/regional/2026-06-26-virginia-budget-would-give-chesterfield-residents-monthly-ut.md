# Virginia Budget Would Give Chesterfield Residents Monthly Utility Rebate

**TL;DR:** Virginia's new two-year budget would return an estimated $3 monthly rebate to residents through revenue from the state's carbon market program.

Virginia lawmakers have approved a two-year budget amendment that would share revenue from the regional carbon market with residents, small businesses, and churches. Under the amendment, utilities that burn carbon energy sources would purchase credits in the market, with 45 percent of those funds returned to customers as an estimated $3 monthly rebate. The budget is currently awaiting review by Governor Abigail Spanberger.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents would receive direct monthly rebates on their utility costs, providing relief on energy bills through the state's emissions reduction program.
