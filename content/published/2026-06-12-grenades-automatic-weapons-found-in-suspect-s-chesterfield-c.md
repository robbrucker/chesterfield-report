# Grenades, automatic weapons found in suspect's Chesterfield County home

**TL;DR:** A Chesterfield County man charged with shooting two police officers and killing a K-9 during a May domestic call had live grenades, fully automatic rifles, and heavy weapons-modification equipment in his home, according to court documents.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Gary G. Shaw, 65, charged with shooting officers and killing police K-9 Knight; Officers Jacob Clark (25) and Jacob Wells (40) injured
- **What:** Live grenades, rifles illegally converted to fully automatic fire, body armor, and weapons-modification equipment found in suspect's home following domestic shooting incident
- **When:** May 31, 2026 (incident); June 11, 2026 (court documents revealing weapons cache)
- **Where:** 12900 block of Gumfork Place, Chesterfield County

## The story

On May 31, Chesterfield County police responded to a domestic disturbance at the 12900 block of Gumfork Place after Shaw's mother called 911 to report her son had fired shots inside the home. She told dispatchers she was hiding in an upstairs bedroom while Shaw threatened to shoot her if she came downstairs. When uniformed officers entered the home to intervene, Shaw fired multiple shots with a long gun, striking two officers and a police canine. K-9 Knight, a five-year-old German Shepherd and Malinois mix who had served with the department for four years, died from his wounds. Officers Jacob Clark, 25, and Jacob Wells, 40, were critically wounded; Clark sustained seven gunshot wounds and underwent multiple surgeries, while Wells was shot in the right bicep and required 71 units of blood transfusions. Shaw was also wounded in the exchange.

Court documents filed in connection with the incident reveal that investigators discovered an extensive weapons cache inside Shaw's home during a search. Police found two rifles illegally converted to function as fully automatic weapons, along with dozens of cartridges and thousands of individual rounds of ammunition. The arsenal also included live grenades, inert pineapple grenades, and live cannonballs. Body armor plates and components for assembling weapons, including AK-47 and AR-15 parts, along with firearms modification manuals, were recovered from the residence.

Of particular concern to investigators was heavy metalworking machinery found in a detached garage, which police say is capable of illegally modifying firearms to fire fully automatically. This equipment, combined with the components and manuals discovered, suggests a deliberate capacity to manufacture illegal weapons. The comprehensive nature of the weapons collection and conversion equipment underscore investigators' concerns about Shaw's weapons expertise and access to dangerous materials.

Shaw now faces 17 felony charges, including two counts of attempted aggravated murder of a law enforcement officer, two counts of aggravated malicious wounding of a police officer, and additional weapons violations related to the illegal modification of firearms and possession of explosives and grenades. His next scheduled court appearance is August 20, 2026. Both wounded officers remain hospitalized in critical but stable condition as of the latest reports.
