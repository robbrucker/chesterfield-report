# Virginia law lets judges order speed-limiting devices for drivers

**TL;DR:** A new Virginia law gives judges the authority to require speed-limiting technology as a condition for some drivers.

Virginia has passed a new law that expands judicial authority over driver safety measures. Under the law, judges can now require certain drivers to install or use speed-limiting technology as part of court-ordered conditions. The law represents a shift in how courts can address traffic safety concerns.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents may face this requirement if convicted of certain driving offenses, as local Circuit Court judges will have the power to impose speed-limiting technology as a condition of driving privileges.
