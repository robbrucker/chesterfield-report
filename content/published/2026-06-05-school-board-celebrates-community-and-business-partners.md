# School Board Celebrates Community and Business Partners

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County School Board recognizes its network of more than 200 community and business partners who support student achievement across the district.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County School Board; 200+ business and community partners
- **What:** School Board celebration and recognition of community and business partners
- **When:** June 2026
- **Where:** Chesterfield County Public Schools, Virginia

## The story

The Chesterfield County School Board gathered in June 2026 to celebrate the collaborative relationships that support student achievement across the district's 67 schools serving over 64,000 students. The recognition ceremony highlighted the more than 200 business and community partners whose involvement spans mentoring, financial support, volunteer efforts, and specialized programming.

Among the most prominent partners recognized are major corporate entities like LEGO, Bon Secours Health System, and TowneBank, which have integrated their expertise and resources into school operations. LEGO provides educational robotics kits across all K-12 schools and supports First LEGO League competitions. TowneBank partners with the Chesterfield Education Foundation on the CCPS Hall of Fame program to honor past employees and volunteers. Bon Secours brings health and wellness initiatives to schools.

Community-based organizations also received recognition. The Midlothian Rotary Club sponsors the annual Students of the Year celebration and maintains year-round literacy and mentoring programs. The Combined Arms Support Command at Fort Lee connects soldiers with schools through mentoring, physical fitness initiatives, leadership programs, and facility improvements including playground and track upgrades. These partnerships extend the reach of school resources into areas from workforce development to early childhood education to student scholarships.

The Chesterfield Public Education Foundation, established in 1989 by community and business leaders, serves as a bridge between schools and their partners. The foundation provides funding that public resources alone cannot, focusing on equitable educational opportunities across the district's 23 Title One schools. Programs include early childhood initiatives, workforce development, the Chesterfield Recovery Academy, literacy engagement through El Librotazo, professional development for teachers, and innovation grants for classroom projects.

## The case for

Community and business partnerships expand educational offerings beyond what taxpayer funding alone can provide. Corporate expertise in robotics, health services, and other fields brings real-world relevance to classrooms. Volunteer mentoring and leadership programs provide personalized student support. These relationships also create local employment pathways and demonstrate how businesses benefit from investing in community education infrastructure.

## The case against

Heavy reliance on private and corporate partnerships can create disparities if some schools attract more partner investment than others, potentially deepening equity gaps. Corporate partnerships may introduce commercial influence or marketing into schools. Dependence on non-public funding sources leaves schools vulnerable when business sponsorships decline. The focus on celebrating partnerships can sometimes obscure inadequate public investment in education itself.

**Why it matters:** The partnership ecosystem allows Chesterfield schools to offer robotics, health programs, mentoring, and innovation grants that would otherwise be unavailable, directly affecting student opportunities and outcomes. Understanding these relationships helps residents see the full picture of who is supporting their schools and how community resources beyond taxes drive educational quality.
