# Virginia Budget Proposal Includes $600M Data Center Fee

**TL;DR:** The Virginia General Assembly is considering a state budget that imposes a $600 million fee on data centers and allocates $7 million for a transitional housing facility in Charlottesville.

On June 22, the Virginia General Assembly convened to debate a state budget proposal with two major components: a $600 million fee targeting data centers and $7 million in funding for a transitional facility serving unhoused residents in Charlottesville. The data center fee represents the state's approach to revenue generation and business regulation, while the housing allocation reflects statewide investment in homelessness initiatives.

**Why it matters:** Statewide tax and regulatory changes, such as the data center fee, can shape business investment decisions and operational costs across Virginia's regions, potentially affecting economic development and jobs locally.
