# Severe Thunderstorm Watch Extended Through 11 PM Tonight

**TL;DR:** The National Weather Service extended a severe thunderstorm watch for Chesterfield County and 20 surrounding central Virginia counties through 11 PM EDT on June 12, warning of damaging winds, heavy rainfall, and possible hail.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** National Weather Service (NWS Wakefield VA)
- **What:** Severe thunderstorm watch extended for 21 Virginia counties
- **When:** June 12, 2026, until 11 PM EDT
- **Where:** Central, east-central, eastern, and north-central Virginia including Chesterfield County

## The story

The National Weather Service extended a severe thunderstorm watch for Chesterfield County and 20 surrounding Virginia counties on June 12, 2026, initially until 9 PM EDT before being extended to 11 PM EDT. The watch covers central, east-central, eastern, and north-central Virginia, affecting areas including Amelia, Henrico, Richmond (city), and numerous municipalities across the region. The extension was issued at 7:29 PM EDT as storm activity continued longer than initially anticipated.

Severe thunderstorms across the region are capable of producing damaging wind gusts—some forecasts mentioning 60 mph winds—along with quarter-size hail, heavy rainfall, and flash flooding in urban and low-lying areas. Minor damage to vehicles and wind damage to trees and powerlines have been reported as storms move through. One severe thunderstorm was tracked moving east at 65 mph near Colonial Heights, illustrating the rapid progression of the weather system.

Chesterfield County, recognized as a StormReady community, has activated its Emergency Management operations to monitor conditions and coordinate response. County residents are urged to tune in to NOAA weather radios, set to alert mode, and monitor local media and social media for the latest updates. The county's emergency preparedness infrastructure includes community alert systems and established protocols for severe weather response, with residents encouraged to move to basements or interior rooms on the lowest floor if tornado conditions develop.

For those in Chesterfield County seeking real-time storm information, the county provides Storm Information updates through its website and mapping portal. Non-emergency public safety concerns can be reported to 804-748-1251, though 911 should be reserved for life-threatening emergencies. Residents are advised to look for danger signs including a dark or greenish sky, large hail, dark low-lying cloud formations with rotation, or a loud roar similar to a freight train—all indicators of potentially severe conditions.

## The case for

Extended watches allow residents more lead time to secure outdoor items, review emergency plans, charge devices, and ensure family members know where to shelter if warnings escalate—preparation that can prevent injuries and property damage during rapidly developing storms.

## The case against

Extended watch windows can lead to warning fatigue if conditions do not materialize as forecast, potentially causing residents to ignore or downplay future alerts; forecasters must balance providing advance notice with maintaining credibility of warnings.

**Why it matters:** Severe thunderstorms with damaging winds and hail pose immediate risks to residents, vehicles, and infrastructure across Chesterfield County. Timely alerts and extended watch periods give residents and county emergency services critical time to prepare and respond, potentially preventing injuries and reducing property damage.
