# Matoaca Softball Makes Historic Run to State Finals

**TL;DR:** Matoaca High School's softball program captured its first state championship in school history, defeating Lightridge 2-1 in a walk-off victory on June 13, 2026.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Matoaca High School Warriors softball team
- **What:** Won Class 5 VHSL state championship with 2-1 walk-off victory over Lightridge
- **When:** June 13, 2026
- **Where:** John Champe High School

## The story

Matoaca High School's softball program wrote its first championship chapter in school history on June 13, 2026, when senior Raven Crabtree delivered a walk-off single in the seventh inning to lift the Warriors past Lightridge 2-1 in the Class 5 VHSL state championship game. The shot to the fence in left center field, which scored pinch runner Zyashia Marbury, capped an extraordinary season for a program that had never before reached the state semifinals, let alone the championship game. Head coach Scott Driskill's Warriors finished the season 26-1.

The victory was built on the strength of pitcher Kaylee Hodges, a junior committed to Tennessee who was a two-time Gatorade State Player of the Year. Hodges allowed just five hits with 17 strikeouts and threw 102 pitches (81 strikes) in the regional semifinal victory over Kellam that secured the Warriors' first-ever regional championship. In the state final, she was nearly untouchable through five and two-thirds innings with no hits allowed and 10 strikeouts before yielding to relief efforts.

The championship game remained scoreless through five innings until Lightridge's Peyton Robinson, a Georgia Tech commit, allowed the pressure to mount. Lightridge scored first on an RBI bloop single by Emmi Harris in the sixth inning. Matoaca answered in the seventh when Abby Davis drove in a run with a groundout to tie the game, setting the stage for Crabtree's heroics.

The historic achievement comes after decades of competitive softball at Matoaca but never reaching this level. Coach Driskill reflected on the magnitude of the moment, emphasizing that his players had accomplished something unprecedented. "Matoaca softball is on the map," he said, capturing the program's arrival as a state title contender.

## Key players

- **Raven Crabtree:** Senior outfielder; hit the game-winning walk-off single in the seventh inning
- **Kaylee Hodges:** Junior pitcher; two-time Gatorade State Player of the Year committed to Tennessee; dominant performance on the mound
- **Scott Driskill:** Head coach of Matoaca softball program
- **Abby Davis:** Player who drove in run to tie the game in seventh inning

## The case for

This championship represents a validation of the program's development and the investment in youth softball in Chesterfield County. It provides visibility and recruitment momentum for future seasons, giving younger players a model of success to aspire to and raising the community profile of girls' athletics in the county.

## The case against

While the achievement is noteworthy, it reflects one exceptional season built around star pitcher Kaylee Hodges, who will graduate. Sustaining this level of success requires continued institutional support, recruitment, and coaching consistency, which can be challenging in public school athletics.

**Why it matters:** For Chesterfield County residents, this milestone signals that the area's young athletes can compete and win at the highest state levels, inspiring future participation in competitive sports while bringing recognition to the county's schools and athletic programs.
