# Motorcyclist seriously injured in Route 288 crash

**TL;DR:** A motorcyclist sustained serious injuries in a single-vehicle crash on Route 288 North near Courthouse Road in Chesterfield County on June 2, 2026.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Motorcyclist (identity undisclosed)
- **What:** Single-vehicle motorcycle crash with serious injuries
- **When:** June 2, 2026 at 5:43 p.m.
- **Where:** Route 288 North at Courthouse Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

A motorcyclist was seriously injured in a single-vehicle crash on northbound Route 288 at Courthouse Road in Chesterfield County on the evening of June 2, 2026. Virginia State Police responded to the scene at 5:43 p.m. and transported the driver to Chippenham Hospital, where the rider received treatment for serious injuries. As of the latest report, the cause of the crash remains under investigation, and no additional details about the circumstances have been released.

Route 288 has been the site of multiple significant traffic incidents in recent months, including a five-person crash in December 2025 near Hull Street, a multi-vehicle collision near Route 1 in October 2025, and another serious crash in April 2026. The corridor, which runs north-south through Chesterfield County connecting Route 1 to I-95, has become a focus for traffic safety and operations improvements through the Virginia Department of Transportation's Route 288 STARS (Study of Transportation Assessment and Recommendations for Safety) study.

Chesterfield County has emerged as one of Virginia's areas with the highest number of motorcycle fatalities. Emergency response capabilities in the area have been enhanced by Chippenham Hospital's designation as a Level I Trauma Center in 2020, enabling immediate access to trauma surgeons and specialized surgical care 24/7. The hospital, in partnership with Chesterfield and Richmond Ambulance, has increased annual trauma activations from 1,500 to over 3,300 patients since earning the designation.

Virginia State Police continues to encourage all drivers to exercise heightened caution on roadways, particularly as warmer weather increases motorcycle use. The investigation into the June 2 crash is ongoing, with a formal police report available through the Virginia Department of Motor Vehicles.

**Why it matters:** The incident highlights ongoing traffic safety concerns on Route 288, a heavily-used corridor in Chesterfield County that has experienced multiple serious crashes in recent months. The rider's transport to a Level I Trauma Center provides optimized emergency care, though the underlying patterns of crashes on the corridor underscore the importance of the ongoing VDOT safety study and potential infrastructure improvements.
