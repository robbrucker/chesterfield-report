# Midlothian Wig Salon Launches Program for Women with Medical Hair Loss

**TL;DR:** Bombshell Wigs Galore, a Midlothian salon owned by Lemeisha Weatherspoon, launched the Bombshell Strong Initiative to support women experiencing hair loss from cancer treatment and other medical conditions.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Bombshell Wigs Galore (Black and woman-owned salon operated by Lemeisha Weatherspoon)
- **What:** Launched Bombshell Strong Initiative to support women with medical hair loss; celebrated 3-year business anniversary
- **When:** June 2026 (Community Crown Day and program launch)
- **Where:** 10617 Hull Street, Midlothian, Chesterfield County, Virginia

## The story

Bombshell Wigs Galore marked its third anniversary in business by launching the Bombshell Strong Initiative, a community program designed to support women experiencing hair loss from cancer treatment and other medical conditions. The Midlothian salon, operated by Lemeisha Weatherspoon, held a Community Crown Day celebration in June to introduce the program and connect residents facing appearance-related challenges with accessible wig solutions and emotional support.

The salon specializes in custom wigs, hair extensions, and professional hair services, serving clients seeking confidence restoration during health crises. According to the salon owner, the initiative creates a safe space for women navigating the emotional and practical impacts of treatment-related hair loss. Client Charlotte Hatfield emphasized the importance of the salon's work, noting that hair plays a significant role in self-image and confidence, particularly during medical treatments.

The Community Crown Day event featured giveaways, refreshments, and a donation box for wig contributions, fostering community connection among attendees. This marks an expansion of the salon's existing community work. In October 2025, Bombshell Wigs Galore hosted a free Wig Confidence and Cancer Awareness Event during Breast Cancer Awareness Month, demonstrating a sustained commitment to serving women undergoing cancer treatment before formalizing the initiative.

The Bombshell Strong Initiative reflects a growing recognition in Chesterfield County of the need for accessible, culturally competent resources for women managing medical hair loss. By combining professional wig services with community support, the salon positions itself as both a business and a resource for residents facing health-related appearance changes.

## The case for

The initiative addresses a real gap in accessible support for women undergoing cancer treatment and other medical conditions that cause hair loss. Professional wig fitting combined with community space and emotional support can meaningfully improve patients' confidence, well-being, and reintegration into daily life during treatment. Supporting a woman-owned, community-focused business that serves this population strengthens local resources and keeps revenue within the Chesterfield community.

## The case against

While the program fills an important need, women with medical hair loss may benefit from complementary support from oncology centers, dermatology practices, or nonprofit cancer organizations that can coordinate care more holistically. The salon's services, while valuable, are a retail solution rather than integrated medical support, so women may need to navigate multiple resources separately. Additionally, wig costs can be significant even with salon support, and insurance coverage for wigs varies widely.

**Why it matters:** The Bombshell Strong Initiative creates a local, accessible resource for Chesterfield County women facing medical hair loss, addressing both the practical and emotional dimensions of appearance changes during cancer treatment and illness. Formalizing the program signals the salon's commitment to serve the broader community beyond routine customers and provides a documented pathway for residents to connect with support.
