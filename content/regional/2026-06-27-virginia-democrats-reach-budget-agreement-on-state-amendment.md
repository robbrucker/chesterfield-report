# Virginia Democrats reach budget agreement on state amendments

**TL;DR:** Gov. Spanberger and state Democratic leaders finalized budget amendments, including one to accept James Monroe's Loudoun County home as a state park.

Virginia Gov. Abigail Spanberger and House and Senate Democratic leaders have resolved budget disputes through a series of amendments. One notable amendment would allow the state to accept the historic Loudoun County home of former President James Monroe as a state park. The agreement signals an end to tensions between the governor and the General Assembly's Democratic majority over the state budget.

**Why it matters:** State budget agreements affect school funding, transportation, and public services that reach Chesterfield County residents, making legislative harmony on spending priorities directly relevant to local budgets and services.
