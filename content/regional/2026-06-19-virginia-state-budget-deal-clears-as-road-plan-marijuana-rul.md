# Virginia State Budget Deal Clears as Road Plan, Marijuana Rules Emerge

**TL;DR:** Virginia budget leaders reached an agreement, while lawmakers weigh recreational marijuana rules and a $29 billion transportation improvement plan.

Virginia state budget leaders have reached an agreement in principle on spending, according to Richmond Times-Dispatch reporting. The state transportation board has authorized a $29 billion multiyear infrastructure improvement plan, while lawmakers are separately considering how recreational marijuana legalization might affect the state's hemp farming industry. The Department of Justice has also challenged certain Virginia laws concerning law enforcement face coverings and ICE agreements.

**Why it matters:** State budget decisions, infrastructure spending, and policy shifts on these issues directly influence local schools, roads, property development, and business conditions for Chesterfield residents.
