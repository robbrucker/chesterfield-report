# Statewide Drought May Impact Data Center Water Use

**TL;DR:** Virginia's extreme drought is forcing water officials to decide whether data centers, which consume hundreds of thousands of gallons daily, will face the same restrictions as other customers.

About one-third of Virginia is under extreme drought conditions, prompting Governor Abigail Spanberger to urge water conservation statewide. As local water authorities impose restrictions on usage, questions are emerging about whether data centers—major consumers of water—will receive exemptions or face the same limits as residents and other businesses. The issue raises broader questions about how to balance supporting the data center industry with protecting limited water supplies during the drought.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield residents' household and business water availability depends on how the county allocates water during drought restrictions, which could affect both supply reliability and water bills.
