# Tall Ships Sail Into Chesterfield for 250th Celebration

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County kicked off its 250th anniversary celebration with tall ships passing Henricus Historical Park on June 11, part of the broader Sail250 Virginia maritime festival.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Parks & Recreation, Virginia Museum of History & Culture, Henricus Foundation
- **What:** Tall ships parade and Richmond SailFest celebration marking America's 250th anniversary
- **When:** June 11, 2026 (ship passage); June 12-14, 2026 (Richmond SailFest)
- **Where:** Henricus Historical Park in Chester and Dock Street Park in Richmond

## The story

Chesterfield County launched its 250th anniversary celebration on Thursday, June 11, as three historic tall ships—Pride of Baltimore II, the Virginia, and the Oosterschelde—sailed up the James River, passing Henricus Historical Park in Chester around midday. At the park, volunteer reenactors in mid-17th century dress fired a ceremonial cannon salute to honor each vessel. The moment marked the beginning of Richmond SailFest, a larger celebration hosted June 12-14 by the Virginia Museum of History & Culture and the City of Richmond at Dock Street Park.

The tall ships voyage is part of Sail250 Virginia, an ambitious 12-day international maritime festival that brought more than 60 tall ships and military vessels from 30 countries to Virginia's shores in June 2026, with over 7,000 visiting sailors, officers, and dignitaries. Eleven Affiliate Harbor events, including Richmond's, welcomed the fleet to communities across the Chesapeake Bay Region. Richmond's segment featured free, family-friendly programming including ship tours (with timed tickets required), live music and performances, historical reenactments, and exhibitions of history and art.

Henricus Historical Park, where Chesterfield residents gathered to witness the ships, sits on the site of the second successful English settlement in North America, established in 1611. The "Citie of Henricus" is home to historic firsts including the charter of the first English college in the New World and the first hospital in colonial North America. Today, the park operates as a living history destination with 14 recreated colonial structures and educational programs.

Beyond the tall ships, Chesterfield County is marking the 250th anniversary with multiple events: a full-day celebration at the County Fairgrounds on July 4, 10 a.m.–8 p.m., featuring living history, reenactments, patriotic music, and fireworks; educational programs exploring Revolutionary War history at local libraries (Thomas Paine and Virginia's Declaration of Rights on June 29; women's roles during the Revolution on July 9); and a new brick memorial walkway and plaza near the Historic Courthouse honoring Revolutionary War soldiers who trained there, known as "The Valley Forge of the South."

## The case for

The tall ships event and broader 250th anniversary programming unite Chesterfield residents around shared national heritage, provide free educational experiences grounded in local history, and highlight the county's deep colonial roots—positioning Chesterfield as a key venue for national commemoration and drawing visitors who support local tourism.

## The case against

Large public events require significant county resources (staff, coordination, infrastructure), and concentrating celebration efforts on one summer weekend may limit accessibility for residents unable to attend during those specific dates. Some may view the focus on early colonial history as emphasizing English settlement while underrepresenting the Powhatan Confederacy and Appomattock communities whose land this was.

**Why it matters:** The 250th anniversary celebrations provide Chesterfield residents with free access to rare living history experiences and educational programming, while positioning the county as a destination for a historic international maritime event. For families, the mix of ship tours, reenactments, and fireworks offers summer activities that connect entertainment to civic heritage.
