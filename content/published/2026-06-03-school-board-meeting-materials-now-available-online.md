# School Board Meeting Materials Now Available Online

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County Public Schools now makes School Board meeting materials, indexed videos, and comprehensive information available online through a centralized portal for public access.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Public Schools; School Board Chair Lisa Hudgins
- **What:** Meeting materials, indexed videos, and School Board information made available online
- **When:** June 2, 2026 business meeting; ongoing transparency resource
- **Where:** chesterfield.gov and bit.ly/CCSchoolBoard

## The story

Chesterfield County Public Schools has made School Board meeting materials, indexed video recordings, and comprehensive School Board information available to the public through a centralized online portal. Residents can now access full meeting agendas, presentations, minutes, and searchable video archives through bit.ly/CCSchoolBoard and the official School Board page on the district website.

The transparency initiative provides multiple ways for residents to stay informed about school district decisions and governance. In addition to online access, the district continues to broadcast School Board meetings live on Comcast channel 98 and Verizon channel 28, and streams meetings online. Archived videos are available through the Chesterfield County Public Schools video archive on Swagit and the district's YouTube channel.

The June 2, 2026 business meeting is one of the regularly scheduled monthly meetings documented in this portal. The school district maintains a consistent meeting schedule, with work sessions beginning at 4 p.m. and business meetings at 6:30 p.m. on the second Monday of most months. Meetings are typically held in the Public Meeting Room at 10001 Iron Bridge Road, and supporting materials are available in advance to allow residents to follow district deliberations.

The School Board is currently led by Chair Lisa Hudgins and Vice Chair Steven Paranto, elected to those positions in 2026. This accessible meeting documentation reflects the district's commitment to public engagement in school governance, allowing residents, educators, and community members to review decisions on matters ranging from budget allocations to policy changes.

## The case for

Online, indexed meeting materials and videos lower barriers to school governance transparency — residents can review discussions on their own schedule, search video content for specific topics, and access supporting documents without attending meetings in person. This deepens civic participation by making district decision-making more discoverable and understandable to working families and those who cannot attend evening meetings.

## The case against

Digital access, while valuable, may deepen an existing engagement gap: residents with time and digital literacy can now easily research School Board decisions, while others may remain unaware of the resource or still struggle with online navigation. Additionally, video archives and indexed materials require ongoing technical maintenance and may not fully capture informal discussion or community input during public comment periods, potentially creating a false sense that the recorded materials tell the complete story of a decision.

**Why it matters:** This transparency resource allows Chesterfield County residents to better understand school district operations, budget decisions, and policy changes that directly affect students and families. Accessible meeting materials can increase community awareness and informed participation in school governance without requiring attendance at scheduled meetings.
