# Virginia may tax data centers' power usage to balance budget

**TL;DR:** Virginia legislators are proposing a new tax on data center electricity consumption to resolve the state's budget shortfall.

Virginia's General Assembly is considering a tax on the power consumed by data centers as one option to break a budget deadlock at the state level. The proposal would generate revenue for the state budget. It's unclear whether the tax would affect existing facilities or apply prospectively.

**Why it matters:** Chesterfield County may host data center operations; a state power tax could affect local business costs and the county's competitiveness in attracting tech infrastructure investment.
