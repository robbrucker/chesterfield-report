# Voting Rights Amendment Pending; Thousands in Registration Limbo

**TL;DR:** Virginia will vote this fall on whether to automatically restore voting rights to people with completed felony sentences, but thousands remain unable to register in the interim.

A constitutional amendment heading to Virginia voters this fall would automatically restore voting rights to people convicted of felonies who have completed their sentences, addressing a long-standing barrier to civic participation. However, thousands of eligible residents currently cannot register to vote because their status remains unclear while the referendum awaits approval. The limbo period leaves these residents unable to participate in local and statewide elections despite having met all legal requirements.

**Why it matters:** The registration delay directly affects thousands of Chesterfield residents who are legally eligible to vote but cannot participate in upcoming federal and local elections while the amendment's fate remains uncertain.
