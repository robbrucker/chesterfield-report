# Teen injured after stepping into traffic near Chesterfield school

**TL;DR:** A teenage girl was transported by helicopter after intentionally stepping into traffic near Thelma Crenshaw Elementary School on Bailey Bridge Road in Midlothian on Tuesday, June 16.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Teenage girl (name not released), driver (remained at scene)
- **What:** Pedestrian struck by vehicle after girl intentionally stepped into roadway
- **When:** Tuesday, June 16, 2026 (evening)
- **Where:** Bailey Bridge Road near Thelma Crenshaw Elementary School, Midlothian, Chesterfield County

## The story

Officers with the Chesterfield County Police Department responded to a crash involving a pedestrian near Thelma Crenshaw Elementary School, located at 11901 Bailey Bridge Road in Midlothian, on Tuesday, June 16, 2026. According to police, a teenage girl intentionally stepped out onto the roadway, where she was struck by a vehicle. The involved driver remained at the scene and cooperated with the investigation.

The teenager sustained injuries serious enough to require emergency air transport. Personnel from Chesterfield County Fire and EMS responded and arranged for the girl to be transported via helicopter from the crash scene. Neither police nor fire could confirm the extent of the girl's injuries to media outlets at the time of reporting.

The incident occurred in a school zone on a road that serves the elementary school, which enrolls students in grades pre-kindergarten through five. Chesterfield County has recently intensified focus on school zone safety, launching a speed enforcement camera program in 2025 to address speeding in areas near schools and adding 12 additional cameras through 2026.

Chesterfield County Police Department is continuing to investigate the circumstances of the incident. Residents with information about the crash are encouraged to contact the Police Department at 804-748-1251.

## The case for

School zone safety infrastructure, including speed enforcement cameras, crosswalk improvements, and traffic calming measures, protects young pedestrians and drivers by reducing speeds and increasing awareness in areas where children are present. Communities nationwide have seen measurable reductions in serious injuries through coordinated safety programs.

## The case against

While safety measures are important, over-reliance on enforcement and cameras may miss the underlying causes of pedestrian incidents involving intentional behavior. Community members sometimes question whether enforcement focuses resources on safety rather than addressing root causes such as youth mental health resources or crisis intervention services available to young people in distress.

**Why it matters:** The incident highlights ongoing concerns about pedestrian safety in school zones and raises awareness about available crisis resources for young people who may be struggling. For families in Chesterfield County, it underscores the importance of understanding both traffic safety and mental health support services, including the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988) and Virginia's mobile crisis response programs for youth experiencing emotional or behavioral crises.
