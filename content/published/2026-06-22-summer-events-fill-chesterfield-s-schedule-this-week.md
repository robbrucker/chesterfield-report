# Summer Events Fill Chesterfield's Schedule This Week

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County residents can enjoy a variety of summer activities this week, including live music, library events, historic tours, and waste disposal services.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation, Chesterfield Public Library, Blair's West Band
- **What:** Summer entertainment events including World Cup watch party, live music concert, historic courthouse tour, and combined waste disposal event
- **When:** Week of June 22-28, 2026 (specific dates vary by event)
- **Where:** Meadowdale Library, Panchita Cline Patio (Summer Nights at the Perk), Historic Courthouse Green, Thomas Dale High School

## The story

Chesterfield County is offering residents a full schedule of summer activities this week, combining entertainment, education, and practical community services. The county's Parks and Recreation department is hosting Summer Nights at the Perk on the Panchita Cline Patio, featuring live music from the local Blair's West Band, a high-energy five-piece group rooted in country music led by husband-and-wife duo Beau and Blair. The free outdoor concert series provides music, food, beverages, and art activities in a casual community setting, with reservations requested.

The Chesterfield Public Library is hosting a World Cup watch party at its Meadowdale Library location (4301 Meadowdale Boulevard, North Chesterfield), giving soccer fans a chance to gather and enjoy the tournament. The library continues its tradition of hosting community events alongside its regular programming.

For history enthusiasts, the Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation department offers guided tours of the Historic Courthouse Green, an outdoor walking tour that explores the historic buildings, monuments, and the 212-year-old Nunnally Oak Tree (planted in 1814 by Lawson Nunnally, assistant deputy clerk of the county's court). Tours focus on discovering the area's fight for religious liberties and its significance to Chesterfield's history.

The county's Waste and Resource Recovery Division is combining three disposal services at Thomas Dale High School (3636 W. Hundred Road, Chester), offering residents a convenient one-stop location to shred personal documents (up to two small boxes or three grocery bags per person, at no fee), recycle electronics, and dispose of household hazardous waste. These combined events reduce the number of trips residents must make to manage different types of waste responsibly.

## Key players

- **Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation:** Host of Summer Nights at the Perk concert series and Historic Courthouse Green tours
- **Chesterfield Public Library:** Host of World Cup watch party at Meadowdale Library
- **Blair's West Band:** Live music performers at Summer Nights at the Perk
- **Chesterfield County Waste and Resource Recovery Division:** Coordinator of shredding, electronics recycling, and hazardous waste disposal event

## Key dates

- **2026-06-21:** Summer Nights at the Perk music series begins (ongoing through summer)
- **2026-06-22:** Summer Fun in the Sun camp begins for children ages 5-12 and teens 13-16
- **2026-08-08:** Household Hazardous Waste special event (next major disposal opportunity after June combined event)

## The case for

Community events like these strengthen neighborhood bonds while offering free or low-cost entertainment. The combined waste disposal event is particularly efficient, allowing residents to manage multiple disposal needs in a single visit, which encourages proper waste handling and reduces illegal dumping. Library and parks programs expand access to cultural activities and recreation regardless of household income, and educational events like courthouse tours preserve local history awareness.

## The case against

Coordinating multiple events requires significant staff and volunteer resources during summer, when departments may already be stretched managing peak seasonal programs, outdoor facility maintenance, and vacation coverage. The timing of these events (staggered dates and venues) may make it harder for some residents to attend, and transportation to scattered locations could be a barrier for those without personal vehicles or in underserved areas of the county.

**Why it matters:** These events provide affordable summer recreation and cultural opportunities for families during the school break, while the waste disposal services help residents responsibly manage materials that require specialized handling, protecting environmental and public health.
