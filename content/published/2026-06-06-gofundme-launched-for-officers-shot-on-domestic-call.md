# Two Chesterfield officers shot; suspect charged, K-9 Knight killed

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County police officers Jacob Clark and Jacob Wells, shot during a May 31 domestic violence response, are recovering from critical injuries as community raises over $60,000 through a GoFundMe campaign.

## The story

On May 31, 2026, Officers Jacob Clark (25, shot seven times) and Jacob Wells (40, shot twice) were wounded when they responded to a domestic disturbance at 12900 Gumfork Place. The suspect's mother had called 911 reporting her son, 65-year-old Gary G. Shaw, had fired shots during a domestic dispute while she barricaded herself upstairs. When officers arrived, Shaw opened fire with both a pistol and long gun; officers returned fire, and all three remained hospitalized. Police K-9 Knight, a five-year-old dog, was killed in the exchange.

Both officers remain in critical but stable condition. Officer Clark received 71 units of blood during treatment and underwent multiple surgeries, while Officer Wells sustained a severe bicep wound and wrist graze. Police Chief Col. Frank Carpenter Jr. noted doctors initially questioned whether they would survive, but both are steadily recovering. A suspicious item—possibly a grenade or cannonball—found at the residence required explosives experts to clear the scene.

A GoFundMe campaign launched by someone connected to one of the officers through Chesterfield Fire and EMS has raised over $60,000 to support medical expenses, rehabilitation, lost work time, and family needs during recovery. The Chesterfield County Police Foundation also established direct officer funds. Community response has been substantial: blood drives organized with Johnston-Willis Hospital filled to capacity despite the high blood-donation requirements, and residents left flowers, cards, and toys at police headquarters in tribute to K-9 Knight.

Gary G. Shaw faces two counts of attempted aggravated murder of a police officer, two counts of aggravated malicious wounding of a police officer, one count of killing a police dog, and four counts of firearm use in commission of a felony. His preliminary hearing is scheduled for August 20, 2026. Both officers bring approximately 7–8 years of service to the department.

## Key dates

- **2026-08-20** — Preliminary hearing for Gary G. Shaw (attempted aggravated murder and related charges)

**Why it matters:** This incident highlights both the physical dangers police face in responding to domestic disturbances—among the highest-risk call types—and how community resources mobilize in crisis. For Chesterfield residents, the shooting raises questions about officer safety protocols, domestic violence intervention strategies, and public support systems for families affected by workplace trauma.
