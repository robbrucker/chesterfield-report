# Virginia Judges Can Order Speed-Limiting Tech for Drivers

**TL;DR:** A new Virginia law gives judges the power to require speed-limiting devices in the cars of drivers convicted of certain offenses.

Virginia has enacted legislation authorizing judges to impose speed-limiting technology as a sentencing condition for drivers convicted of specific violations. The devices restrict a vehicle's maximum speed, adding a tech-based enforcement tool to the state's traffic safety and criminal justice options. This measure applies statewide, including cases heard in Chesterfield County courts.

**Why it matters:** Local judges now have a new discretionary tool to address dangerous driving behavior among convicted offenders, potentially reducing speeding-related accidents and injuries in Chesterfield communities.
