# Downed Power Lines Close Iron Bridge Road in Chester

**TL;DR:** A vehicle crash knocked down power lines on Iron Bridge Road in Chester, leaving over 500 homes without power.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Over 500 residents near Meadowbrook High School
- **What:** Vehicle struck utility pole, downing power lines and closing road
- **When:** April 7, 2022
- **Where:** Iron Bridge Road, Chester, Chesterfield County, Virginia

## The story

A vehicle struck a utility pole on Iron Bridge Road in Chester on April 7, 2022, downing power lines and closing eastbound lanes of the road while traffic was diverted to westbound lanes. The impact left more than 500 homes in the surrounding area without power, primarily near Meadowbrook High School.

An adult male driver was transported to the hospital with life-threatening injuries from the crash. Dominion Energy utility crews responded to clear the downed lines and restore power to affected residents.

The incident highlighted the vulnerability of major roadways in Chesterfield County to disruption from vehicle accidents involving utility infrastructure, which can cascade into widespread outages affecting hundreds of households.

## Key players

- **Dominion Energy:** Utility company responding to restore power
- **Chesterfield County Emergency Services:** First responders

## The case for

Downed power lines from accidents are rare events; most Chesterfield residents experience reliable power. The quick response from Dominion Energy and emergency services minimized the duration of outages and restored service efficiently.

## The case against

Single-vehicle accidents can cause infrastructure failures affecting hundreds of residents. Undergrounding power lines in high-traffic areas like Iron Bridge Road or installing additional protective barriers could reduce vulnerability, though at significant cost and construction disruption.

**Why it matters:** Power outages affecting hundreds of households disrupt daily life, impact vulnerable residents (elderly, those on medical equipment), and can have cascading effects on schools and businesses. The incident underscores infrastructure resilience concerns for Chesterfield residents.
