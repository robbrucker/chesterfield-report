# New Trail and Parking at Winterpock Area Park Coming This Fall

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County Parks is opening a new 10-space parking lot, 1.5-mile wooded trail loop, and paved pedestrian path at Winterpock Area Park in fall 2026.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation
- **What:** Construction of new parking lot (10 spaces), 1.5-mile trail loop, and paved pedestrian/bike path
- **When:** Fall 2026
- **Where:** Winterpock Area Park, 13501 Beach Road, Chesterfield

## The story

Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation is expanding outdoor recreation options in the western part of the county with a new park development at Winterpock Area Park (also known as Beach Road Park), located at 13501 Beach Road. The project adds a 10-space parking lot, a 1.5-mile wooded trail loop, and a paved pedestrian and bicycle path along Beach Road near the Brandy Oaks neighborhood. These amenities are designed to serve residents in one of the fastest-growing residential areas of Chesterfield County.

The Winterpock area has experienced significant residential growth in recent decades, particularly since the 1970s, and now features primarily owner-occupied single-family homes. The new park infrastructure reflects the county's effort to keep pace with the recreational needs of expanding communities in this southwest section of Chesterfield.

The project's completion is contingent upon Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT) approval of a signalized crosswalk that will allow park users to safely cross Beach Road to access the trailhead. Once approved, the park is targeted to open in fall 2026. Chesterfield County currently operates 67 parks with 44 miles of trails across the county, and this new facility will add to those recreation options.

## Key players

- **Chesterfield County Parks and Recreation:** Project manager and developer of the recreational amenities

## Key dates

- **Fall 2026:** Winterpock Area Park scheduled to open (subject to VDOT crosswalk approval)

## The case for

The park expands recreational access for a rapidly growing residential community, providing families and residents with safe, free outdoor amenities including walking and cycling infrastructure. The 1.5-mile loop trail offers accessible fitness options in a wooded setting, while the paved pedestrian path along Beach Road improves multimodal transportation connectivity to the neighborhood.

## The case against

The 10-space parking lot is modest and may be insufficient on peak usage days, potentially limiting accessibility for residents without direct trail access. The project's timeline dependency on VDOT approval introduces uncertainty, and construction costs relative to capacity or maintenance obligations are not publicly detailed.

**Why it matters:** This park addresses outdoor recreation needs in one of Chesterfield's fastest-growing residential areas, enhancing quality of life and providing safe, free access to nature for families and active residents. The addition of pedestrian and cycling infrastructure supports county sustainability goals while serving a growing population.
