# State approves hospital expansion doubling Chesterfield facilities

**TL;DR:** Virginia approved $672 million in hospital expansion across three healthcare systems in Chesterfield County, adding 166 beds and doubling the county's hospital capacity by 2030.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** VCU Health, HCA Healthcare, Bon Secours Healthcare System; Virginia State Health Commissioner B. Cameron Webb
- **What:** Three hospital expansion projects approved by Virginia health officials: 66-bed new VCU Health hospital, 60-bed HCA Magnolia Hospital, and 40-bed expansion of Bon Secours St. Francis Medical Center
- **When:** Approved March 13, 2026; completions scheduled 2029–2030
- **Where:** Chesterfield County, Virginia (VCU Health: Iron Bridge/Nash roads area; HCA: Hull Street/Otterdale roads in Moseley; Bon Secours: Charter Colony Parkway in Midlothian)

## The story

Virginia State Health Commissioner B. Cameron Webb approved three competing hospital expansion projects in Chesterfield County on March 13, 2026, representing a $672 million combined investment that will add 166 new hospital beds to the county. The approvals mark a historic moment: all three healthcare systems—VCU Health, HCA Healthcare, and Bon Secours—received simultaneous approval despite competing proposals, a rare occurrence in Virginia's regulated hospital market. The projects respond to rapid population growth that has added nearly 37,000 residents to Chesterfield County since 2020, bringing the county population to approximately 401,300.

VCU Health will build its first hospital in the county—a 203,000-square-foot, 66-bed facility at the intersection of Iron Bridge and Nash roads near the county government complex, scheduled to open in May 2030. The hospital will include 42 medical/surgical beds, 6 pediatric beds, 6 obstetric beds, 12 ICU beds, and specialized services including a cardiac catheterization lab and MRI imaging. HCA Healthcare is developing the 60-bed Magnolia Hospital on Hull Street and Otterdale roads in the Moseley area by October 2029, featuring 54 medical/surgical beds, 6 ICU beds, four operating rooms, and imaging services. HCA plans to relocate beds and surgical capacity from existing Richmond-area hospitals and close its Swift Creek freestanding emergency department once the new facility opens.

Bon Secours Healthcare System will add a 40-bed expansion—36 medical/surgical and 4 ICU beds—to its existing St. Francis Medical Center on Charter Colony Parkway in Midlothian, completing by mid-September 2029. This two-story, 58,400-square-foot addition will grow St. Francis from 185 beds to 225 beds, following a previous $108 million expansion that added 55 beds. Notably, Bon Secours filed legal challenges to the approvals in April 2026, claiming the competing projects were unnecessary; HCA and VCU Health responded in June 2026 by seeking dismissal of the appeals in Chesterfield Circuit Court.

The state health board had initially endorsed only the Bon Secours and VCU Health projects in October 2025, recommending denial of HCA's Magnolia Hospital, but an adjudication officer's February 2026 review reversed course and recommended approval of all three. Commissioner Webb's final decision approved all three, noting they had "considerable community support." The expansions will effectively double the number of hospitals serving Chesterfield County residents, with two entirely new facilities launching alongside the expansion of an existing major medical center.

## The case for

The three-hospital expansion directly addresses documented population growth and existing healthcare capacity constraints in one of the region's fastest-growing counties. Adding 166 beds spreads access across three competing healthcare systems, potentially improving choice and competition while reducing wait times and overcrowding. VCU Health's entry marks the first time it operates a hospital in the county; combined with HCA and Bon Secours investments, residents gain options previously unavailable, and the competition may drive quality improvements and service innovation. The projects support local job creation during construction and ongoing operations, with all three facilities opening before 2031.

## The case against

Three competing hospital expansions—each with substantial capital investment and overhead—may result in duplicated services, underutilized beds, and higher operating costs than a coordinated regional approach. HCA's plan to relocate beds from other Richmond hospitals suggests capacity shuffling rather than net new market growth; if demand does not materialize as projected, the county could face oversupply and financial strain on all three systems. The legal challenge filed by Bon Secours reflects genuine concern about sustainability: approving all three competing projects simultaneously, rather than prioritizing based on need, assumes unlimited demand growth. Long-term financial viability depends on population growth meeting projections and payers adequately compensating hospitals for the new capacity.

**Why it matters:** These approvals determine where Chesterfield residents receive hospital care for the next decade and signal state confidence in the county's growth trajectory and healthcare need. The new capacity addresses documented shortages in obstetric and pediatric services while the competitive approval process may improve service quality and patient choice, though ongoing legal challenges create uncertainty about construction timelines and final project scope.
