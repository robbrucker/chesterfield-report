# Driver Seriously Injured in Crash on Route 288

**TL;DR:** A driver was seriously injured when their vehicle overturned on Route 288 north of Courthouse Road in Chesterfield County on June 11.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** One driver (unidentified)
- **What:** Single-vehicle crash with vehicle rollover; serious non-life-threatening injuries
- **When:** June 11, 2026, 12:24 p.m.
- **Where:** Route 288 north of Courthouse Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

On Thursday, June 11, at approximately 12:24 p.m., Virginia State Police responded to a single-vehicle crash on Route 288 north of Courthouse Road in Chesterfield County. The vehicle overturned during the incident, and the driver sustained serious, but non-life-threatening injuries. Emergency responders transported the injured driver to a local hospital. The investigation into the crash remains ongoing, with state police not yet disclosing the cause.

The crash forced the closure of the south right lane and right shoulder of Route 288 during the afternoon, affecting traffic in the area. This incident marks the second serious crash in the same corridor within days—on June 3, a motorcyclist was seriously injured in a single-vehicle crash near Courthouse Road.

The Route 288 corridor through Chesterfield County has long been associated with safety concerns. Residents have complained about speeding, aggressive driving, and heavy congestion during peak travel times. Data from Chesterfield County government documents indicate a pattern of crashes in the Route 360/Route 288 area, prompting both enforcement and infrastructure improvements.

To address these concerns, Chesterfield County and VDOT are implementing Streamline 360/288, a multi-year initiative with nine projects aimed at improving safety and traffic flow. Key improvements include widening Route 288 from four to six lanes between Powhite Parkway and Commonwealth Centre Parkway, constructing a northbound flyover ramp, and enhancing the Route 360/Route 288 interchange. In addition to infrastructure work, law enforcement conducted a focused traffic enforcement operation on Route 288, issuing 122 citations for speeding, impaired driving, aggressive driving, and seatbelt violations.

## Key players

- **Virginia State Police** — Emergency response and investigation of the crash
- **Chesterfield County** — County jurisdiction; developing safety improvement initiatives
- **VDOT (Virginia Department of Transportation)** — Traffic monitoring and long-term corridor safety improvements

**Why it matters:** The crash highlights ongoing safety challenges on a major regional corridor that thousands of Chesterfield residents use daily. With multiple serious incidents in recent weeks and longstanding congestion issues, the incident underscores the importance of both immediate enforcement and the longer-term Streamline 360/288 infrastructure improvements meant to reduce crashes and improve travel times.
