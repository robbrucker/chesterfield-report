# West Ridge High School Reaches Construction Milestone

**TL;DR:** Chesterfield County celebrated the structural completion of West Ridge High School, its first new high school in 20 years, with a topping-off ceremony on June 16, 2026.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Public Schools and community leaders
- **What:** Topping-off ceremony marking completion of structural work on West Ridge High School
- **When:** June 16, 2026
- **Where:** West Ridge High School, Matoaca District (Duval Road), Chesterfield County

## The story

School and county leaders celebrated a key milestone in West Ridge High School's construction on June 16, 2026, with a traditional topping-off ceremony. The event marked the placement of the final structural beam, a construction tradition symbolizing progress toward completion. As part of the ceremony, workers hoisted an evergreen tree (representing good luck and continued growth), a broom (symbolizing safety and a "clean sweep" with no worker injuries), and the American flag atop the structure. Officials and attendees signed the beam before it was lifted into place, speaking about new beginnings and the community's shared commitment to supporting students and families.

West Ridge High School is the first new high school built in Chesterfield County in 20 years, with the previous new facility, Cosby High School, opening in 2006. The two-story school covers more than 340,000 square feet and is designed to accommodate up to 2,400 students in grades 9 through 12. The project carries a total cost of $196 million and is funded through the 2022 Community Facilities Bond, which Chesterfield voters approved in November 2022 with a $540 million package for schools, public safety, libraries, and parks.

The school is strategically located in the Matoaca District near Deep Creek Middle School (which opened in August 2025) and Deep Creek Elementary (opening August 2026), helping serve growing enrollment in the western portion of the county. Dr. Elizabeth Baber was named principal of the new school. Completion of structural work positions the project on track for its scheduled opening in August 2027.

By 2029, bond-funded school facilities are expected to add 8,779 total seats countywide: 3,531 in elementary schools, 2,848 in middle schools, and 2,400 in the new high school. This capacity expansion reflects Chesterfield's ongoing need to serve students even as overall K-12 enrollment has plateaued in recent years, with a slight decline of 131 students from 2022 to 2025.

## The case for

The new high school addresses critical capacity needs in Chesterfield's western corridor and eliminates reliance on portable classrooms in that region. With 2,400 new seats, West Ridge will modernize educational infrastructure for a generation of students, offering updated facilities, technology, and spaces that older schools cannot match. The school's opening also validates the 2022 bond investment and delivers on a voter mandate approved with broad support.

## The case against

The $196 million investment is substantial in an era when countywide K-12 enrollment has declined slightly (down 131 students between 2022 and 2025) and some existing schools operate below capacity. Questions about true long-term enrollment demand, maintenance costs for a large new facility, and whether capital could have been better directed to repairs and upgrades at existing schools warrant consideration alongside the growth projections that drove the school's approval.

**Why it matters:** West Ridge High School represents a major commitment to school capacity and modernization in Chesterfield, addressing enrollment growth in the western part of the county and eliminating portables in that area. The milestone signals the county's ability to execute major bond-funded projects on schedule and deliver facilities that serve students for decades to come.
