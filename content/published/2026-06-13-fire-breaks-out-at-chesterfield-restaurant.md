# Fire breaks out at Chesterfield restaurant

**TL;DR:** A fire broke out Saturday morning at Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant in a Hull Street Road shopping center in Chesterfield County, but was extinguished quickly with no injuries.

## Quick facts

- **Who:** Chesterfield County Fire and EMS
- **What:** Structure fire at Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant
- **When:** Saturday, June 13, 2026, approximately 9:35 a.m.
- **Where:** 11100 block of Hull Street Road, Chesterfield County

## The story

A fire broke out at Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant on Saturday morning, June 13, prompting a quick response from Chesterfield County Fire and EMS crews. The incident was reported around 9:35 a.m. at a location in the 11100 block of Hull Street Road, where the restaurant operates in a shopping center.

When firefighters arrived on scene, they found smoke billowing from the roof of the building with a small fire burning inside the restaurant. The fire department moved quickly to contain and extinguish the blaze, bringing it under control in less than 20 minutes. No injuries were reported as a result of the incident.

The cause of the fire was not immediately detailed in initial reports. The extent of damage to the restaurant's interior and whether it will affect future operations remained unclear in the immediate aftermath.

## Key players

- **Chesterfield County Fire and EMS:** Emergency responders who extinguished the fire
- **Yen Ching Chinese Restaurant:** The business where the fire occurred

**Why it matters:** The swift response by Chesterfield County Fire and EMS prevented a potentially serious incident from spreading further, protecting both the business and nearby properties in the shopping center. The incident underscores the importance of rapid emergency services in commercial areas.
