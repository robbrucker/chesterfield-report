# Virginia's healthcare centers warn reporting mandate threatens vulnerable patient access

**TL;DR:** Virginia's proposed state budget would impose new administrative reporting requirements on community health centers without state funding to support them.

The Community Healthcare Association has raised concerns that new 340B drug pricing reporting mandates included in Virginia's state budget would create unfunded administrative work for community health centers statewide. The association argues these compliance costs could force the centers to divert resources from direct patient care. The group is urging the governor to reconsider requirements that lack budget allocations to cover implementation costs.

**Why it matters:** Community health centers in Chesterfield provide primary care and services to lower-income and uninsured residents; increased administrative costs without funding could limit their ability to expand access to care in the county.
